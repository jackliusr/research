## 1. Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS)

**Status:** Publicly-funded autonomous university, also offers self-financing programmes.

### Intake Periods

| Programme Type | Intakes | Months |
|---------------|---------|--------|
| Full-time Undergraduate | **1 intake per year** | **July** only |
| Part-time Undergraduate | **2 intakes per year** | **January** and **July** |
| Graduate Programmes | Varies by programme | Typically Jan, Jul; some also Apr/Oct |

### Application Deadlines

- **Full-time UG (Jul 2026 intake):** Applications open ~January, close ~March. Fixed deadline (not rolling).
- **Part-time UG (Jan 2027 intake):** Applications open ~August 2026, close ~October 2026.
- **Part-time UG (Jul 2026 intake):** Applications open ~January 2026, close ~March 2026.
- **Self-financing programmes:** May have later deadlines; check individual programme pages.

### How to Apply

- **Portal:** SUSS Admissions Portal (online only at suss.edu.sg)
- **Documents required:** Academic transcripts, NRIC/passport, personal statement, 2 referees (for some programmes)
- **English proficiency:** IELTS 6.5–7.0 / TOEFL 85–100 (varies by programme)
- **Application fee:** SGD 10–20 (varies)

### Processing Time

- **4–8 weeks** for undergraduate applications
- Admission offers typically released 8–12 weeks after deadline

### Special Notes

- Full-time UG has **only one intake per year** — missing the deadline means waiting a full year
- Indicative Grade Profile (IGP) published annually for reference
- Part-time students enjoy more flexibility with 2 intakes

## 2. SIM Global Education (SIM GE)

**Status:** Singapore's largest private education provider. Offers programmes from 10+ partner universities including University of London (LSE, Goldsmiths, UCL), University of Wollongong, RMIT, University of Birmingham, University at Buffalo (SUNY), La Trobe University, University of Stirling, and more.

### Intake Periods

| Partner University | Typical Intake Months |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| **University of London** (LSE, Goldsmiths, UCL) | Apr, Jul, Oct |
| **University of Wollongong** | Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct |
| **RMIT University** | Feb, Jul |
| **University of Birmingham** | Sep/Oct |
| **University at Buffalo (SUNY)** | Jan, Aug |
| **La Trobe University** | Feb, Jul, Oct |
| **University of Stirling** | Sep, Jan |

> **Key pattern:** Most SIM GE programmes offer **2–3 intakes per year**. Business programmes tend to have more frequent intakes than specialised ones.

### Application Deadlines

- **Rolling admissions** for most programmes
- Recommended to apply **6–8 weeks before intake start date**
- International students should apply **2–3 months before** to allow student pass processing
- Confirmed dates from past cycles: Feb, May, Jul, Aug, Sep, Oct intakes are common

### How to Apply

- **Portal:** SIM Connect / SIM application portal (simge.edu.sg)
- **Via agent:** SIM works with approved education counsellors
- **Documents required:** Academic transcripts, English proficiency scores, passport copy, passport photo, application form
- **English proficiency:** IELTS 5.5–6.5 (varies by partner and programme level); TOEFL/PTE also accepted
- **Application fee:** SGD 100–150 (varies)

### Processing Time

- **2–6 weeks** from completed application to offer letter
- University of London programmes may take 4–8 weeks (external processing)

### Foundation / Diploma vs Degree Timelines

- **SIM Foundation Studies:** Feb, Apr, Jul, Oct intakes
- **SIM Diploma:** Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct intakes
- **SIM-University of London Degrees:** Apr, Jul, Oct intakes
- **SIM-University of Wollongong:** Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct

### Late Application Policy

- Late applications accepted up to **2 weeks before intake** for most programmes
- International student pass applications may not be processed in time for late applicants

## 3. James Cook University Singapore (JCU)

**Status:** Full Australian university branch campus. Operates on a trimester system identical to JCU Australia.

### Intake Periods

| Trimester | Months |
|-----------|--------|
| **Trimester 1** | **March – June** |
| **Trimester 2** | **July – October** |
| **Trimester 3** | **November – February** |

> All three trimesters are available for most undergraduate and postgraduate programmes.

### Application Deadlines

- **Rolling admissions** for all trimesters
- Recommended deadlines:
  - **Trimester 1 (Mar start):** Apply by **end of January**
  - **Trimester 2 (Jul start):** Apply by **end of May**
  - **Trimester 3 (Nov start):** Apply by **end of September**
- International students: Apply **2–3 months before** trimester start

### How to Apply

- **Portal:** JCU Singapore online application (jcu.edu.sg)
- **Documents required:** Certified transcripts, English proficiency results, passport, CV (for postgraduate)
- **English proficiency:** IELTS 6.0–6.5 / TOEFL 74–86 (varies by programme)
- **Application fee:** SGD 0 (free to apply for most programmes)

### Processing Time

- **2–4 weeks** for standard applications
- Conditional offers can be issued within 1 week for strong applicants

### Special Notes

- Trimester 3 (Nov) is popular for students who miss the July intake
- Foundation programme also follows same trimester pattern
- Some programmes (e.g., Psychology, Veterinary) have limited intakes
- Late applications: Accepted but may need to defer to next trimester for visa processing

### Intake Periods

| **University of Essex** | Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct |

> Kaplan is known for having **the most frequent intakes** — often 6+ per year for popular programmes.

### Application Deadlines

- **Rolling admissions year-round**
- Applications accepted up to **4 weeks before intake** for local students
- International students should apply **8–10 weeks before** desired intake
- No fixed cut-off dates — Kaplan actively encourages year-round applications

- **Documents required:** Transcripts, English test scores, passport/IC, passport photo

### Foundation / Diploma vs Degree

- **Kaplan Diploma:** 6+ intakes per year (Jan–Oct, monthly)
- **Kaplan Degree (Murdoch, etc.):** 4–6 intakes (Jan, Mar, May, Jul, Sep, Oct)
- **Kaplan Graduate Certificates/Diplomas:** 2–4 intakes

- Very flexible; late applications accepted up to **2 weeks before start date**

## Special Categories & Cross-Cutting Notes

### 1. Rolling Admissions vs Fixed Deadlines

| Institution | Model |
|------------|-------|
| SUSS (FT UG) | **Fixed deadline** — only 1 intake/yr |
| SUSS (PT UG) | Rolling (but limited intakes) |
| Most private universities (SIM, Kaplan, PSB, MDIS, etc.) | **Rolling admissions** — apply anytime |
| JCU, Curtin (branch campuses) | Rolling but trimester-based |
| All smaller PEIs (TMC, EASB, Raffles, etc.) | Rolling admissions |

> **Takeaway:** Almost all private universities in Singapore use rolling admissions. The key constraint is not the deadline but the **intake start date** and **student pass processing time** for international students.

### 2. English Language Requirements

| Programme Level | Typical IELTS | Typical TOEFL | Typical PTE |
|----------------|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| Foundation / Certificate | 5.0–5.5 | 40–60 | 36–46 |
| Diploma | 5.5 | 46–60 | 42–50 |
| Bachelor's Degree | 6.0–6.5 | 70–86 | 50–64 |
| Master's Degree | 6.5–7.0 | 86–100 | 58–70 |

> Most institutions accept **IELTS Academic**, **TOEFL iBT**, and **PTE Academic**. Some accept **Cambridge English (CAE/CPE)** and **MUET**.
> Kaplan, PSB, MDIS, and others offer **in-house English proficiency programmes** as alternatives.

### 3. Foundation/Diploma vs Degree Timelines

| Pathway | Typical Duration | Intake Frequency |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|
| Foundation programme | 6–8 months | 3–4 intakes/yr |
| Diploma | 8–12 months | 3–6 intakes/yr |
| Bachelor's Degree | 18–36 months | 1–4 intakes/yr |
| Master's Degree | 12–18 months | 1–3 intakes/yr |

> **Important:** Foundation/diploma programmes tend to have **more frequent intakes** than degree programmes. Students planning a pathway should check whether the intake schedule aligns.

### 4. Late Application Policies

| Institution | Late Application Policy |
|------------|------------------------|
| SIM GE | Accepted up to 2 weeks before intake; visa risk for int'l students |
| JCU | Accepted but may need deferral for visa processing |
| Curtin | Late applications considered case-by-case |
| Kaplan | Most flexible — up to 1–2 weeks before or even mid-month |
| PSB Academy | Accepted up to 2–3 weeks before; conditions apply |
| MDIS | Late applications considered case-by-case |
| SUSS (FT) | **Not accepted** — fixed deadline strictly enforced |
| All others | Generally flexible but int'l students should not rely on late apps |

> **Warning:** Late applications for international students carry significant visa risk. Always apply at least 2 months before the intake start date.

### 5. International Student Pass Processing

| Stage | Estimated Time |
|-------|---------------|
| Document preparation | 1–2 weeks |
| Application to offer letter | 2–6 weeks |
| Student Pass application (ICA) | 4–8 weeks |
| **Total recommended lead time** | **8–12 weeks before intake** |

### 6. Certificate of Registration (CPE) Status

All institutions listed above are registered with the **Committee for Private Education (CPE)**, part of SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG). Registration status can be verified at the SSG website. Always check that:
- The institution has **valid registration**
- The programme is **CPE-accredited**
- The **Fee Protection Scheme (FPS)** is in place

## How to Use This Guide

1. **Identify your target institution(s)** from the Master Table
2. **Check the next available intake** and calculate your application window
3. **Account for student pass processing** (international students: add 8–12 weeks)
4. **Prepare documents** — transcripts, English test scores, passport
5. **Apply online** or through an authorised agent
6. **Confirm with the institution** — deadlines can change without notice
7. **Apply to multiple institutions** as backup — most have free or low-cost applications
