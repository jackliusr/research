# Universities Recognized by NTU and NUS for Graduate Admissions

**A Comprehensive Guide for Prospective Graduate Applicants** | *Last Updated: June 2026*

---

## 1. The Recognition System Explained

### What Is It?
NUS (National University of Singapore) and NTU (Nanyang Technological University), with Singapore's Ministry of Education (MOE), maintain internal categorizations of overseas universities for graduate admission. The system classifies universities into tiers based on institutional quality, global reputation, research output, and accreditation standards. An applicant's undergraduate institution tier directly affects minimum GPA requirements, GRE/GMAT waiver eligibility, English language waiver eligibility, and scholarship considerations.

### Why Does It Exist?
- **Volume**: NUS/NTU receive tens of thousands of applications annually from 100+ countries. A standardized system allows fair evaluation.
- **Grade comparability**: A 3.5/4.0 from one university may be top-of-class; from another, merely average. Tiers adjust expectations.
- **Quality assurance**: Singapore's autonomous universities need to screen institutions whose degrees may not meet their standards.
- **Regulatory alignment**: The MOM COMPASS framework maintains a related list for Employment Pass purposes.

### How the Tier System Works
While exact classifications are internal, the general structure is:
- **Tier 1 (Top Tier)**: Ivy League, Oxbridge, top Russell Group, top Go8, C9 League (China), IITs, top global 100. Lowest minimum GPA.
- **Tier 2 (Mid Tier)**: Most Russell Group, most Go8, Project 985 Chinese universities, NITs/IIITs, top public universities. Higher minimum GPA.
- **Tier 3 (Standard Tier)**: Recognized accredited universities. Highest minimum GPA or additional requirements.
- **Not Recognized**: Universities not on the internal list. Generally ineligible unless exceptional qualifications or a recognized master's.

### Public vs. Internal Lists
The lists are **NOT publicly available** in complete form. Closest proxies:
- **MOM COMPASS C2 List**: [MOM PDF](https://www.mom.gov.sg/-/media/mom/documents/work-passes-and-permits/compass/compass-c2-list-of-top-tier-institutions.pdf)
- **NUS GDA2 System**: When applying, select university from a dropdown. If yours isn't there, it's likely not recognized.
- **NTU Portal**: Similarly, NTU's portal has a pre-populated university list.

### Who Maintains Them?
- **NUS**: Office of Admissions (OAM) + NUS Graduate School (NUSGS), in consultation with individual faculties.
- **NTU**: Graduate Studies Office, in consultation with colleges/schools.
- **MOE**: General guidance on international degree equivalency.
- **Individual Faculties**: Different faculties may maintain their OWN sub-lists.

## 2. NUS Recognition Framework

### Classification System
NUS internally classes overseas universities into tiers:
- **Group A (Top Tier)**: Globally elite — top 100 QS/THE/ARWU, Ivy League, Oxbridge, C9 League, IIT Bombay/Delhi/Madras/Kharagpur/Kanpur.
- **Group B (Recognized)**: Well-established — most Russell Group, most Go8, most Project 985, top national universities.
- **Group C / Non-Recognized**: Not on the recognized list.

### How Tier Affects Requirements

| Tier | Min GPA (4.0) | Chinese % | Indian CGPA |
|------|--------------|-----------|-------------|
| Group A | 3.0-3.2 | 80-85/100 | 7.5-8.0/10 |
| Group B | 3.2-3.5 | 85-88/100 | 8.0-8.5/10 |
| Non-Recognized | Not eligible | — | — |

**GRE/GMAT**: Group A + GPA > 3.5 usually waived. Group B typically requires GRE/GMAT. NUS MBA rarely waives. NUS Computing increasingly requires GRE for all non-Singapore applicants.

**English**: Auto-waiver for UK/USA/Australia/NZ/Canada/Ireland degrees. Conditional waiver for Singapore, India (English-medium), Hong Kong. No waiver for China, Japan, Korea, most of Europe, Middle East, Latin America.

### Programs Using Recognition
- **MSc/MA/MBA**: Heavily tier-dependent.
- **MPhil/MEng Research**: Moderately tier-dependent; research can compensate.
- **PhD**: Moderately tier-dependent; publications and supervisor fit matter heavily.
- **Professional Doctorates**: Similar to PhD.

### Faculty Differences

| Faculty | Strictness | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Engineering (FoE) | Moderate | ABET-accredited. IELTS min 6.0. |
| Computing (SoC) | Strict | Increasingly requires GRE. Selective on recognition. |
| Business School | Very Strict | MBA rarely accepts non-top-tier. GMAT 680+ expected. |
| Science (FoS) | Moderate | Research can offset tier. |
| Arts & Social Sciences | Moderate | Department-dependent. |
| Law | Extremely Strict | Top law schools only (Harvard, Yale, Oxford, Cambridge, etc.). IELTS 7.5. |
| Medicine (Yong Loo Lin) | Very Strict | Recognized medical schools only. |
| Duke-NUS Medical School | Very Strict | US-style MD. MCAT required. |

### NUS GDA2 System
The **NUS Graduate Admission System (GDA2)** is the online portal at https://gradadmissions.nus.edu.sg/. Key features:
- Pre-populated university dropdown — if your university doesn't appear, it's likely not recognized.
- Degree validation and document upload.
- Application tracking.
**Source**: [NUS Graduate Admissions](https://www.nus.edu.sg/oam/graduate-admissions)

### NUS Group B (Recognized) Universities
Full list is internal, but generally includes: all QS/THE/ARWU Top 200, all C9 League, most Project 985, all IITs, IISc Bangalore, top ASEAN universities, all Russell Group, all Go8, all Ivy League, top US publics (UCB, UCLA, UMich, UIUC), top European tech (ETH Zurich, EPFL, TU Delft, TUM).

## 3. NTU Recognition Framework

### Classification
NTU's system aligns closely with: global rankings, accreditation bodies (ABET, AACSB), and national classification systems (C9/985/211 for China, IIT system for India).

### Requirements Impact
Same pattern as NUS: tier affects min GPA, English waivers, GRE/GMAT, work experience (for professional programs).

### Differences Across NTU Colleges

| College/School | Strictness | Notes |
|---------------|-----------|-------|
| College of Engineering | Moderate | ABET important. 6 schools (CEE, EEE, MAE, MSE, SCSE, IGS). |
| College of Science | Moderate | SPMS, SBS, ASE, CCEB. Research important. |
| Nanyang Business School (NBS) | Strict | AACSB. GMAT 650+. |
| CoHASS | Moderate | Portfolio for arts. |
| LKCMedicine | Very Strict | Joint with Imperial. Recognized med schools only. |
| NIE | Moderate | Teaching experience valued. |
| RSIS | Moderate | International affairs experience valued. |

### China "Big 4" Recognition at NTU
- **Tier 1**: Tsinghua, Peking, Fudan, Shanghai Jiao Tong.
- **Tier 1 Extended**: Zhejiang, Nanjing, USTC.

- **Tier 3**: Other 985 / Double First-Class.

### Automatic Waiver
If your undergraduate degree was completed at a university physically located in: **UK, USA, Australia, New Zealand, Canada** (except French-language institutions), **Ireland**.

### Test Validity
IELTS/TOEFL/PTE valid for **2 years** from test date. Must be valid at time of application.

## 10. Application Impact

### Minimum GPA Requirements by Tier

| Background | Min GPA (4.0) |
|-----------|--------------|
| Tier 1 University | 3.0-3.2 |
| Tier 2 University | 3.2-3.5 |
| Tier 3 University | 3.5-4.0 |
| Not recognized | Not eligible |

For **competitive programs** (CS, Data Science, Finance, Law, Medicine), add 0.2-0.5 to these minimums.

### GRE/GMAT Waiver Eligibility

| Scenario | GRE/GMAT Required? |
|---------|-------------------|
| Tier 1 + GPA > 3.5 | Usually waived |
| Tier 1 + GPA 3.0-3.5 | May be waived |
| Tier 2 + GPA > 3.7 | May be waived |
| Tier 2 + GPA < 3.7 | Typically required |
| Tier 3 + any GPA | Typically required |
| MBA (any university) | Almost always required |

Some programs (NUS Master of Computing, Data Science) now REQUIRE GRE for ALL applicants.

### Scholarship Eligibility
Tier significantly affects scholarship consideration:
- **NUS Research Scholarships**: ~70-80% of funded PhD positions go to Tier 1 graduates.
- **NTU Research Scholarship**: Same pattern.
- **SINGA (Singapore International Graduate Award)**: Tier 1 strongly preferred.
- **NUS MBA Scholarships**: Tier 1-2 prerequisite for merit-based scholarships.
- **A*STAR, EDB scholarships**: Favor Tier 1 graduates.

### Admission Priority
1. **Priority 1**: Tier 1 + strong GPA + strong test scores
2. **Priority 2**: Tier 1 + avg GPA OR Tier 2 + strong GPA
3. **Priority 3**: Tier 2 + avg GPA OR Tier 3 + strong GPA
4. **Priority 4**: Tier 3 + avg GPA (most competitive)
5. **Not eligible**: Non-recognized

### Is a Recognized University Mandatory?
**For most programs, YES.** Limited exceptions:
- **Research exception**: Multiple top publications can overcome recognition.
- **Master's exception**: Recognized Master's after unrecognized Bachelor's can qualify.
- **Professional experience exception**: MBA/Executive programs with 10+ years experience.
- **Exceptional talent**: Olympiad medalists, startup founders (rare).

**These exceptions are RARE. Do not rely on them.**

## 11. Common Misconceptions

### 1. "My university is well-known so it must be recognized."
**False.** A university famous in your country may be unknown to NUS/NTU. Examples: BITS Pilani (famous in India, not equal to IITs for NUS/NTU); Chinese non-985/211 universities; many US state universities.

### 2. "NUS/NTU publish their full list."
**False.** The lists are internal tools. Closest public proxy is the MOM COMPASS C2 list, but even that isn't identical.

### 3. "I can appeal to have my university added."
**Very difficult.** Lists are reviewed periodically (every 1-3 years) based on rankings changes, accreditation, and track record of previous applicants. Individual appeals are almost never successful.

### 4. "Rankings determine recognition."
**Only partially.** NUS/NTU also consider: historical track record, accreditation (ABET, AACSB), specific discipline reputation, government recognition, and partnerships. A university ranked #250 with strong ABET accreditation may be better recognized than one ranked #150.

### 5. "Graduating from a recognized university guarantees admission."
**Absolutely false.** Recognition only sets the baseline. You still need competitive GPA (top 10-20%), strong test scores, excellent recommendations, compelling SOP, research (for research programs), and work experience (for professional programs). Thousands from recognized universities are rejected yearly.

### 6. "All Russell Group universities are treated equally."
**False.** Oxford/Cambridge/Imperial/LSE/UCL are Tier 1. Warwick/Edinburgh/KCL/Manchester/Bristol are Tier 1-2. Others are Tier 2. Queen's Belfast/Cardiff are often Tier 2-3.

### 7. "A recognized Master's fixes an unrecognized Bachelor's."
**Depends.** For PhD: a recognized Master's CAN serve as qualifying degree. For Master's applications: an unrecognized Bachelor's is very difficult to overcome. Some programs look at ALL degrees.

### 8. "Online/distance degrees from recognized universities are treated the same."
**False.** NUS/NTU typically do NOT recognize online/distance learning degrees unless the program had significant on-campus attendance.

### 9. "Graduates from unrecognized universities never get in."
**Very difficult but not impossible.** Exceptions for outstanding publications, exceptional professional achievement, joint programs, or recognized Master's degree.

### 10. "The recognition system is the same for all Singapore universities."
**False.** Each autonomous university (NUS, NTU, SMU, SUTD, SIT, SUSS) maintains its own list. They overlap significantly but are not identical.

## 12. How to Check If Your University Is Recognized

### Method 1: Email Admissions Directly (Most Reliable)
**For NUS**: gradadmissions@nus.edu.sg | Faculty-specific emails available.
**For NTU**: gradmatters@ntu.edu.sg | College-specific emails available.

**Email Template:**
```
Subject: University Recognition Inquiry for Graduate Admission
Dear Sir/Madam,
I am planning to apply for [Program] at [NUS/NTU]. Could you please confirm whether [University Name] in [Country] is on the recognized list for this program? I would also appreciate any information on minimum GPA requirements.
Thank you.
Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

### Method 2: Use NUS GDA2 System
1. Go to https://gradadmissions.nus.edu.sg/
2. Start a new application (no need to submit).
3. In Education section, type your university name.
4. If it appears in autocomplete: it's in NUS's database (still check with faculty).

### Method 3: Use NTU Portal
1. Go to https://admissions.ntu.edu.sg/
2. Same process as NUS GDA2.

### Method 4: Check with Specific Faculty
Different faculties may have different lists. Contact the department directly with your transcript.

### Method 5: Consult Education Agents
Reputable agents: **IDP Education Singapore** (https://www.idp.com/singapore/), **AEI Study Abroad**, **JACK Study Abroad**. Cross-reference with at least one other method.

### Method 6: Research Previous Successful Applicants
Check: **Reddit** (r/NUS, r/NTU, r/gradadmissions), **The GradCafe** (https://www.thegradcafe.com/), **Yocket** (https://yocket.com/ — popular for Indian applicants), **Zhihu** (https://www.zhihu.com/ — popular for Chinese applicants), **LinkedIn**.

### Method 7: MOM COMPASS C2 List
The MOM list at https://www.mom.gov.sg/-/media/mom/documents/work-passes-and-permits/compass/compass-c2-list-of-top-tier-institutions.pdf partially overlaps with NUS/NTU recognition.

### Method 8: Global Ranking Proxies (Rough Heuristics)
- **QS/THE/ARWU Top 200**: Almost certainly recognized (Tier 2+).
- **QS 200-500**: Likely recognized (Tier 3+).
- **QS Below 500**: May or may not be recognized.
- **ARWU Top 300**: Likely recognized.

**Disclaimer**: These are rough proxies. Only direct NUS/NTU confirmation is definitive.
