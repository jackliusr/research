## Executive Summary

Singapore's private education landscape consists of ~260 registered Private Education Institutions (PEIs), with ~180 actively delivering courses to ~150,000 students. The sector is regulated by the Committee for Private Education (CPE) under SkillsFuture Singapore.

**Key findings:**
- **Best-in-class (private):** SIM Global Education, James Cook University Singapore — EduTrust Star, largest facilities, strongest partnerships
- **Most affordable:** Kaplan ($18-26k/yr), PSB ($20-30k/yr), MDIS ($16-28k/yr)
- **Foreign campuses:** JCU Singapore and Curtin Singapore offer degrees identical to their Australian parent universities
- **Gap with public:** Private grads earn ~$3,100/mo median vs $4,200/mo for public uni grads; employment rates ~58% vs 84%
- **Key regulatory changes:** Enhanced Registration Framework (2024) raised standards, closed ~30 weaker PEIs
- **International recognition:** Only JCU Singapore and local autonomous universities are automatically recognized by China's Ministry of Education

## 1. Regulatory Framework

### 1.1 Committee for Private Education (CPE)

- Regulates all PEIs under **SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG)**
- Enforces the **Enhanced Registration Framework (ERF)** since 3 January 2024
- ERF requirements: minimum paid-up capital, mandatory graduate employment data submission, compliance with Industry Competency Frameworks, stricter course approval

### 1.2 EduTrust Certification (Voluntary Quality Assurance)

| Tier | Meaning | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| **EduTrust Star** | Outstanding quality, robust self-improvement | SIM Global Education, James Cook University Singapore, Curtin Singapore |
| **EduTrust** | Meets core criteria | Kaplan, PSB Academy, MDIS, Raffles College |
| **EduTrust Provisional** | New entrants, minimum requirements, 1-2 yr validity | Select institutions |
| **No certification** | Cannot recruit international students | ~80 of 260 registered PEIs |

- ~180 of ~260 registered PEIs hold EduTrust as of 2025

### 1.3 Fee Protection Scheme (FPS)

- **Mandatory** for all registered PEIs
- Two mechanisms: **insurance** (via Lonpac or approved provider) or **bank escrow**
- Protects student fees if PEI closes or fails to deliver course

### 1.4 Enhanced Registration Framework (2024) Impact

- ~30 PEIs lost registration or suspended operations
- Increased industry consolidation; larger players (SIM, Kaplan, PSB) gaining market share
- Higher barriers to entry for new PEIs

### 7.2 China Ministry of Education Recognition

| Institution | China MoE Recognized? | Notes |
|-------------|----------------------|-------|
| **NUS, NTU, SMU, SUTD, SIT, SUSS** | ✅ Yes | Autonomous universities |
| **JCU Singapore** | ✅ Yes | Recognized as full foreign campus |
| **Curtin Singapore** | ⚠️ Conditional | Generally accepted but not automatic |
| **SIM partner degrees** | ❌ Not automatic | May be rejected for civil service/regulated professions |
| **Kaplan partner degrees** | ❌ Not automatic | Depends on partner university's recognition in China |

**Impact:** Chinese students intending to return to China for regulated professions (government, finance, law) should choose JCU Singapore or a public AU.

### 7.3 MOM Employment Pass (COMPASS) Recognition

- MOM maintains a list of **recognized universities** for bonus points under the COMPASS framework
- Local AUs and JCU Singapore are on the list
- Private partnership degrees may get fewer COMPASS points unless the partner university itself is top-ranked
- Practical impact: private degree grads can still get EP but need higher salary to compensate for fewer points

### 7.4 Professional Body Accreditation

Many PEI degrees are accredited by professional bodies:
- **ACCA, CIMA, CPA Australia** — for accounting programs (SIM, Kaplan, PSB)
- **CFA Institute** — for finance programs (SIM)
- **IET, CEng** — for engineering programs (PSB-Newcastle)
- **BPS** — for psychology programs (JCU, Kaplan-Murdoch)

| **Working professional (part-time study)** | Kaplan or SIM (flexible schedules) | Multiple intakes, evening/weekend options |
| **Design/fashion/arts student** | Raffles, LASALLE, or NAFA | Specialized programs, industry connections |
| **MBA/Executive student** | NUS, NTU, SMU (local), INSEAD (global elite) or S P Jain (multi-campus) | FT-ranked programs, global recognition |
| **Engineering/STEM focused** | PSB Academy (Newcastle engineering) or SIT (public) | Strong labs, industry partnerships |
| **Fast degree completion** | Kaplan or SIM (accelerated 16-24 months) | Trimester system, year-round study |

### 8.2 Pros & Cons of Private Education

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Lower entry requirements — accessible to diploma holders | Higher tuition costs for locals (no subsidy) |
| Accelerated programs (2-3 years vs 3-4 at AUs) | Lower graduate employment rates |
| Foreign university brand (UoL, RMIT, Newcastle) | Weaker employer recognition vs NUS/NTU |
| Flexible scheduling (part-time, evening, online) | Limited campus facilities vs AUs |
| Multiple intakes per year | Variable faculty quality (partner-dependent) |
| Wide range of partnerships and specializations | Smaller alumni networks |
