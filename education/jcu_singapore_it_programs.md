## 1. Institutional Overview

James Cook University Singapore (149 Sims Drive, Singapore 387380) is the **wholly owned branch campus** of James Cook University Australia, established in 2003. It is one of the few foreign universities in Singapore granted the title "University" (not "institute" or "centre") and holds the prestigious **EduTrust Star** — the highest level of EduTrust certification awarded by SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG), signifying exemplary quality assurance in the private education sector.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Established** | 2003 |
| **Location** | 149 Sims Drive, Singapore 387380 |
| **Parent University** | James Cook University Australia (ranked top 2% worldwide — ShanghaiRanking ARWU) |
| **EduTrust** | EduTrust Star (highest tier) |
| **QS Stars 2024** | 5 Stars for Teaching, Research, Facilities, Employability, Good Governance, Environmental Impact, Global Engagement, and Overall |
| **QS World Ranking 2025** | #415 (JCU Australia) |
| **Student Body** | ~4,000 students (highly international) |
| **Class Sizes** | Small — typically 20–40 students per class |
| **Academic Year** | Trimester system: **March (SP1), July (SP2), November (SP3)** |

## 2. Undergraduate IT Programs

JCU Singapore offers four undergraduate IT-related degrees and one diploma pathway. All undergraduate degrees are **AQF Level 7** qualifications, identical to what JCU Australia awards.

### 2.1 Bachelor of Information Technology (BIT)

**CRICOS Code:** 074639K  
**Duration:** 24 months full-time (accelerated via trimesters) | 36–48 months part-time  
**Total Subjects:** 24 subjects (72 credit points)  
**Majors (choose one):**

| Major | Focus Area |
|---|---|
| Cyber Security | Network security, ethical hacking, digital forensics, risk management |
| Interactive Technologies & Games Design | Game development, 2D/3D graphics, interactive visualisation, UX |
| Business Informatics | Business intelligence, enterprise systems, data-driven decision making |
| Internet of Things | Embedded systems, sensor networks, IoT architecture, smart systems |
| Data Science | Big data analytics, machine learning, data visualisation, statistical modelling |

**Curriculum Structure (24 subjects):** ~10–12 core subjects + ~6–8 major subjects + ~4–6 electives (max 10 Level 1 subjects).

**Intake:** March (SP1), July (SP2), November (SP3) — trimesters<br>**Fees:** ~SGD 29,500–33,000 per year (~SGD 59,000–66,000 total)<br>**Degree Awarded:** Bachelor of Information Technology (same as JCU Australia)

### 2.2 Bachelor of Cybersecurity

**Duration:** 24 months full-time | Part-time available  
**Total Subjects:** 24 subjects  
**Focus:** A dedicated cybersecurity degree — one of the few in Singapore offered by a QS-ranked university. Includes partnerships with **KPMG, ISACA, CREST, and Red Hat Academy**.  
**Key Subjects:** Network Infrastructure & Security, Ethical Hacking & Penetration Testing, Digital Forensics, Cryptography, Risk Management & Governance, Machine Learning for Security, Security Operations, Cyber Law & Ethics  
**Intake:** March, July, November  
**Fees:** ~SGD 59,000–66,000 total  

### 2.3 Bachelor of Science (Majoring in Data Science)

**Duration:** 24 months full-time  
**Total Subjects:** 24 subjects  
**Focus:** Statistical analysis, machine learning, big data infrastructure, data visualisation  
**Key Subjects:** Data Mining, Machine Learning, Big Data Analytics, Data Visualisation, Statistical Modelling, Programming for Data Science, Database Systems, Cloud Computing for Data  
**Articulation:** Can articulate into BEng (Hons) in Electronic Systems and IoT Engineering at JCU Cairns with only 2 additional years  
**Intake:** March, July, November  

### 2.4 Bachelor of Science (Majoring in Internet of Things)

**Duration:** 24 months full-time  
**Focus:** Embedded systems, sensor networks, IoT architecture, smart systems design  
**Key Subjects:** IoT Architecture, Embedded Systems, Sensor Networks, Wireless Communications, Data Analytics for IoT, Cloud & Edge Computing  
**Articulation:** Pathway to BEng (Hons) in Electronic Systems and IoT Engineering at JCU Cairns  
**Intake:** March, July, November  

### 2.5 Diploma of Higher Education (Information Technology)

**Duration:** 8 months full-time (2 trimesters)  
**Total Subjects:** 8 subjects  
**Purpose:** A pathway program for students who do not meet direct entry requirements for the BIT.  
**Articulation:** Upon completion, students receive **advanced standing of 8 subjects** into the BIT (i.e., Year 1 is waived, enter directly into Year 2).  
**Intake:** March, July, November

## 3. Postgraduate IT Programs

All postgraduate programs are **AQF Level 9** qualifications.

### 3.1 Master of Information Technology (MIT)

**Duration:** 12–16 months full-time | 24–36 months part-time  
**Total Subjects:** 12–16 subjects (depending on major)  
**Majors (choose one):**

| Major | Focus |
|---|---|
| Computing | Software development, networking, systems administration |
| Data Science | Big data analytics, machine learning, statistical modelling |
| Cyber Security | Network security, ethical hacking, digital forensics, security governance |
| Interactive Technologies & Games Design | Game development, AR/VR, interactive media |
| Business Informatics | Business intelligence, enterprise systems, IT strategy |
| Artificial Intelligence | *(newer major)* Machine learning, deep learning, NLP, AI ethics |
| Software Engineering | Full-stack development, testing, mobile programming, SDLC |

**Intake:** March, July, November  
**Fees:** ~SGD 36,000–42,000 total (SGD 20,000–21,000 per year)  
**Degree Awarded:** Master of Information Technology (same as JCU Australia)

#### MIT Curriculum Overview (12-16 subjects)

| Component | Subjects |
|---|---|
| Foundation (if non-IT background) | 4 foundation subjects (may be waived with relevant degree/experience) |
| Core | 4–6 subjects covering research methods, IT project management, professional practice |
| Major | 4 subjects in chosen specialisation |
| Elective | 2–4 subjects |
| Capstone / Project | 1–2 subjects (ICT Project / Industry Placement / Thesis) |

**Foundation Subjects (for non-IT background students):**  
- Problem Solving & Programming 1  
- Problem Solving & Programming 2  
- Database Modelling  
- Web Design & Development  

**Entry Requirements for Advanced Standing:**  
- 4 foundation subjects may be waived for applicants with:
  - A cognate undergraduate degree (IT, Computer Science, Information Systems), OR
  - Minimum 4 years full-time equivalent professional work experience in ICT sector

### 3.2 Master of Data Science

**Duration:** 12–16 months full-time  
**Focus:** Advanced data analytics, machine learning, big data infrastructure, research methods  
**Key Subjects:** Data Mining, Machine Learning, Big Data Analytics, Statistical Data Analysis, Data Visualisation, Research Methods for IT, Capstone Project  
**Intake:** March, July, November  
**Fees:** ~SGD 36,000–42,000 total  

### 3.3 Graduate Certificate / Graduate Diploma Pathways

**Articulation Ladder:** Graduate Certificate (GC) → Graduate Diploma (GD) → Master's

| Level | Subjects | Duration (Full-Time) | Duration (Part-Time) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Graduate Certificate of Information Technology | 4 subjects | 4 months | 8 months |
| Graduate Diploma of Information Technology | 8 subjects | 8 months | 16 months |
| Master of Information Technology | 12–16 subjects | 12–16 months | 24–36 months |

- **GC entry:** Bachelor's degree in any field  
- **GD entry:** Completion of GC or equivalent  
- **Master's entry:** Completion of GD or direct entry with Bachelor's degree  
- Students can exit early with the lower qualification if they choose not to complete the full Master's

### 3.4 MIT-MBA Joint Degree

**Duration:** 16–20 months full-time  
**Structure:** Combined Master of Information Technology and Master of Business Administration  
**Fees:** Premium pricing over standalone degrees  
**Target:** Professionals seeking both technical depth and management breadth

## 6. Intake & Duration

| Program | Intake Months | Full-Time Duration | Part-Time Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bachelor programs | **March, July, November** | 24 months (8 trimesters) | 36–48 months |
| Diploma of HE (IT) | **March, July, November** | 8 months (2 trimesters) | 12–16 months |
| Master of IT | **March, July, November** | 12–16 months (4–5 trimesters) | 24–36 months |
| GC / GD pathways | **March, July, November** | 4–8 months | 8–16 months |

**Trimester Calendar (Typical):**
- **SP1:** March – June (exams in June)
- **SP2:** July – October (exams in October)
- **SP3:** November – February (exams in February)

The trimester system enables **year-round study** with no long summer break, allowing degrees to be completed in 2/3 of the time of a traditional 3-year program.

## 7. Entry Requirements

### Undergraduate (Bachelor's Programs)

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Academic** | Satisfactory completion of 12 years of schooling (or equivalent). Accepted qualifications: GCE A-Level (2 passes), Singapore Polytechnic Diploma, IB Diploma, Australian ATAR, Indian Standard XII (≥60%), or equivalent. |
| **English Proficiency** | **IELTS (Academic): 6.0** (no band below 6.0). Alternatives: TOEFL iBT 74 (no band < 18), Pearson PTE Academic 52 (no band < 52), or JCU English Language Preparatory Program (ELPP) completion. |
| **Subject Prerequisites** | None specifically required for BIT. For BSc Data Science — some mathematical background recommended. |
| **International Qualifications** | Country-specific requirements published by JCU. Typical: Chinese Gaokao (≥60%), Indonesian SMA (≥80%), Malaysian STPM (≥C+), Vietnamese Grade 12 (≥7.0). |

### Postgraduate (Master's Programs)

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Academic** | Bachelor's degree in any field from a recognised university. IT-background or cognate degree recommended for some majors. Non-IT degree holders take 4 foundation subjects. |
| **Work Experience** | 4+ years of relevant ICT experience may waive foundation subjects for MIT applicants. |
| **English Proficiency** | **IELTS (Academic): 6.5** (no band below 6.0). Alternatives: TOEFL iBT 86 (no band < 21), PTE Academic 58 (no band < 54). |
| **Degree Classification** | Typically GPA 4.0/7.0 or equivalent. Specific requirements vary by stream. |

### Pathway Programs

- **Pre-University Foundation Program:** For students who have completed GCE O-Level or equivalent (8 months → articulates into Year 1 of Bachelor's)
- **Diploma of Higher Education (IT):** For students who narrowly miss direct entry (8 months → advanced standing of 8 subjects into BIT)
- **Postgraduate Qualifying Program (Business):** For students who do not meet direct Master's entry requirements

## 8. Credit Exemptions & Advanced Standing

JCU Singapore is generous with credit exemptions, particularly for diploma holders from Singapore polytechnics.

| Background | Typical Exemption | Equivalent to |
|---|---|---|
| **Singapore Polytechnic Diploma (IT-related)** | 8–16 subjects (up to 50% of degree) | 1–2 years of the BIT |
| **JCU Diploma of HE (IT)** | 8 subjects | Year 1 of BIT (full exemption) |
| **Other recognised diplomas** | 4–12 subjects (assessed case-by-case) | Varies |
| **A-Level / IB (high scores)** | 0–4 subjects (very limited) | Core subject exemptions |
| **Relevant work experience (adults)** | 0–4 subjects (case-by-case) | Foundation subjects |
| **Previous university studies** | Up to 16 subjects (with detailed syllabus matching) | Up to 2 years |

**Advanced Standing Guidelines:**
- Maximum 16 subjects (50%) of the BIT can be exempted
- Subjects are assessed using the Advanced Standing Assessor tool on the JCU website
- Exempted subjects are graded as "CR" (Credit) and do not affect GPA
- Students must complete at least 50% of the degree at JCU Singapore for the degree to be awarded
- Polytechnic diploma holders from Singapore can typically complete the BIT in **12 months (1 year)** with 16 subjects exempted

## 10. Teaching & Learning Model

- **Trimester system:** 3 trimesters/yr (Mar, Jul, Nov); ~12 teaching wks + 1 exam wk each; enables 2-yr Bachelor's, 12–16 mo Master's
- **Class sizes:** Small — 20–40 students per class
- **Faculty:** Mix of JCU Australia professors (fly-in/fly-out) + locally hired PhD-qualified academics with industry experience
- **Teaching methods:** Lectures, tutorials, lab sessions, flipped classrooms, online components
- **Assessment:** Mid-trimester & final exams (30–50%), programming projects, reports, presentations, portfolios, capstone projects
- **Practical labs:** IT computer labs, dedicated **cybersecurity lab** (ethical hacking/pen testing/forensics), **AWS Academy** cloud curriculum, financial lab (Bloomberg terminals)
- **Campus (149 Sims Drive):** Lecture theatres, seminar rooms, PC & Apple labs, library with full JCU Australia digital access, student hub, basketball courts, gym, prayer room, café
- **Inter-campus exchange:** Study at JCU Cairns/Townsville (Australia) for up to 2 trimesters paying Singapore fees

### Graduate Employment Outcomes

From the **JCU Singapore Graduate Employment Survey 2023–2024** (conducted by Blackbox Research; target 1,180 graduates, ~20% response rate):

### Where JCU Excels

- **Accelerated completion:** 2-year Bachelor's (vs 3–4 at AUs)
- **Own campus + EduTrust Star:** Highest SSG quality tier
- **ACS Professional accreditation** for all IT programs
- **Same degree as Australia** — identical parchment
- **Industry partnerships:** AWS Academy, Red Hat, KPMG, ISACA, CREST
- **Inter-campus exchange:** Study at JCU Australia paying Singapore fees
- **Small class sizes (20–40)** vs AU lecture halls of 50–200

### Where JCU Falls Short

- **Lower global ranking (#415)** vs NUS #8, NTU #26 — weaker brand signalling
- **Teaching-focused Singapore campus** — limited research intensity
- **Premium pricing** vs subsidised local AUs
- **Variable employer recognition** — some government/GLC roles prefer AU graduates
- **Less curriculum depth** from 2-year accelerated model vs 4-year degrees
- **Student Pass restrictions** — no work during term (ICA)
- **No on-campus housing** — students arrange private rental

## 13. Notes for Working Professionals

### Part-Time Study Options

JCU Singapore offers **Continuing Education (part-time)** programs at the Bachelor's and Master's levels. The same curriculum is delivered over a longer period:

| Program | Full-Time Duration | Part-Time Duration | Class Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bachelor of Information Technology (Part-Time) | 24 months | 36–48 months | Evening classes (typically 6:30 PM – 9:30 PM) |
| Master of Information Technology (Part-Time) | 12–16 months | 24–36 months | Evening classes |
| Graduate Certificate / Diploma (Part-Time) | 4–8 months | 8–16 months | Evening classes |

- Part-time students take **2 subjects per trimester** (vs 4 for full-time)
- Classes are scheduled in the evenings to accommodate working hours
- Same degree awarded as full-time students (no notation of part-time mode)
- Part-time students on Student Pass must still attend ≥90% of classes (if applicable)

### SkillsFuture & Government Subsidies

| Scheme | Eligibility | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **SkillsFuture Credit (SFC)** | Singapore Citizens aged 25+ | SGD 500–4,000 credits for approved courses. Check myskillsfuture.gov.sg if JCU programs are SSG-supported. |
| **Mid-Career Enhanced Subsidy (MCES)** | Singapore Citizens aged 40+ | Up to 90% subsidy for SSG-supported courses and MOE-subsidised programmes. |
| **SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme (SFLP)** | Singapore Citizens aged 40+ | Additional SGD 4,000 credit. Includes a Mid-Career Training Allowance for full-time training. |
| **CPF Education Scheme** | Singapore Citizens / PR | Use CPF Ordinary Account savings to pay part-time degree fees. Applicable for approved part-time programs. |
| **NTUC Union Training Assistance Programme (UTAP)** | NTUC members | Up to SGD 500 per year for union-approved courses. |

> **Note:** Not all JCU programs are SSG-supported. Singapore Citizens / PR should verify eligibility on myskillsfuture.gov.sg or contact JCU's admissions office directly about subsidised part-time slots.

### Employer Sponsorship

- MNCs and large Singapore employers (especially banks, consultancies, and tech firms) often sponsor part-time degrees
- Typical sponsorship covers tuition fees (full or partial), sometimes with a bond (1–2 years)
- JCU's Careers Services can provide supporting documentation for employer sponsorship applications
- JCU is on many corporate training provider panels (check with your employer's L&D department)

### Time Commitment & Networking (Part-Time)

- **Time commitment:** ~12–15 hrs/week (2 subjects); ~6–8 hrs (1 subject)
- **Student clubs:** IT Society, Cybersecurity Club, interest groups
- **Industry events:** Career fairs, industry talks, networking at Sims Drive campus
- **SCS events:** Through SCS institutional membership
- **Alumni network:** JCU Alumni (global, Singapore chapter)
- **Careers Services:** Employment workshops, CV clinics, mock interviews

### Post-Study Visa Considerations

- **LTVP (Long-Term Visit Pass):** Available for PR/SPR graduates
- **Employment Pass:** Fresh graduates can apply (min S$5,000/mo threshold in 2025)
- **Student Pass rules:** ≥90% attendance; no work during term; max 16 hrs/week during holidays
- **S-Pass:** Lower threshold (S$3,150) — achievable with JCU median salary
