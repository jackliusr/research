## 1. Introduction & Overview

Singapore's higher education landscape comprises **seven autonomous universities (AUs)** funded by the Ministry of Education (MOE). These institutions are publicly governed but enjoy autonomy in academic, administrative, and financial matters. Each university has a distinct mission, ranging from world-class research (NUS, NTU) to applied/practice-oriented education (SIT, SUSS) and specialised arts (UAS).

**The Seven Autonomous Universities at a Glance:**

| # | University | Abbr. | Founded | Location | Students (approx.) | QS 2025 Rank | THE 2025 Rank |
|---|-----------|-------|---------|----------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | National University of Singapore | NUS | 1905 | Kent Ridge, Bukit Timah, Outram | ~40,000 | #8 | #19 |
| 2 | Nanyang Technological University | NTU | 1981 (as NTI); 1991 (as NTU) | Yunnan Garden (Jurong West) | ~33,000 | #15 | #30 |
| 3 | Singapore Management University | SMU | 2000 | Bras Basah (City) | ~12,000 | #585 | #301-350 |
| 4 | Singapore University of Technology & Design | SUTD | 2009 | Changi | ~2,000 | #461 | #351-400 |
| 5 | Singapore Institute of Technology | SIT | 2009 (uni 2014) | Dover (HQ) + 5 poly satellites | ~10,000 | #1001+ | #801-1000 |
| 6 | Singapore University of Social Sciences | SUSS | 2017 (as uni) | Clementi | ~15,000 | Not ranked | Not ranked |
| 7 | University of the Arts Singapore | UAS | 2024 | City (LASALLE/NAFA merge) | ~5,500 (est.) | Not ranked | Not ranked |

**Key Facts:**
- **Total AU enrolment (2025):** ~120,000 students across all 7 universities
- **MOE Budget for AUs (FY2025):** ~S$8.5 billion (recurrent + development)
- **Tuition Grant Scheme (TGS):** All Singaporean undergraduates receive MOE subsidy. A 3-year bond is required for PRs and international students who take the grant, requiring them to work in Singapore after graduation.
- **Admission:** Predominantly via A-Levels, Polytechnic Diplomas, IB, NUS High School Diploma, and international qualifications. Aptitude-based admissions are available at all universities.

## 2. National University of Singapore (NUS)

### A. Profile & History

**History:**
NUS traces its roots to 1905 when the Straits Settlements and Federated Malay States government founded the **Straits Settlements and Federated Malay States Government Medical School** (later King Edward VII College of Medicine). In 1949, Raffles College (founded 1928) and King Edward VII College merged to form the **University of Malaya**. In 1962, the Singapore division became the **University of Singapore**, which merged with Nanyang University in 1980 to form the **National University of Singapore**.

**Campuses:**
- **Kent Ridge (Main Campus):** 150 hectares in southwest Singapore. Houses the Faculties of Arts & Social Sciences, Science, Engineering, Computing, Design & Engineering, NUS College, and most central admin. Features University Town (UTown) — a dedicated residential and learning precinct completed in 2011.
- **Bukit Timah Campus:** Houses the Faculty of Law and the Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy. Located near Botanic Gardens.
- **Outram Campus:** Duke-NUS Medical School, located within the Singapore General Hospital (SGH) campus.

**Academic Structure (16 Faculties/Schools):**
- Faculty of Arts & Social Sciences (FASS)
- Faculty of Science
- Faculty of Engineering
- School of Computing
- NUS Business School
- Faculty of Law
- Yong Loo Lin School of Medicine
- Faculty of Dentistry
- Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy (LKYSPP)
- Yong Siew Toh Conservatory of Music
- Duke-NUS Medical School (graduate-level, joint with Duke University)
- Institute of Systems Science (ISS) — graduate continuing education
- College of Design & Engineering (CDE) — formed 2022 merging Engineering and Design & Environment
- NUS College (NUSC) — honours liberal arts college, replacing University Scholars Programme (USP) and Yale-NUS College from AY2022/23

**Student Population:**
- **Undergraduate:** ~31,000
- **Graduate:** ~9,000 (research + coursework)
- **Total:** ~40,000

**Rankings (2025/2026):**
- **QS World University Rankings 2025:** #8 (highest in Asia)
- **THE World University Rankings 2025:** #19
- **ARWU (Shanghai) 2024:** #68
- **QS Subject Rankings 2025 highlights:**
  - Petroleum Engineering: #1
  - Civil Engineering: #2
  - Architecture/Built Environment: #5
  - Computer Science: #6
  - Engineering (Electrical/Electronic): #8
  - Social Sciences: #6
  - Law: #10
  - Medicine: #21
- **THE Subject Rankings 2025:**
  - Business & Economics: #11
  - Education: #12
  - Engineering: #11
  - Law: #12
  - Physical Sciences: #15
  - Clinical & Health: #21

**Notable Alumni:**
- **Heads of State/Government:** Lee Hsien Loong (PM of Singapore, 2004–2024), Goh Chok Tong (PM, 1990–2004), Tony Tan (President of Singapore), Tharman Shanmugaratnam (President of Singapore, 2023–), Halimah Yacob (President, 2017–2023), Abdullah Badawi (PM of Malaysia, 2003–2009)
- **Business:** Min-Liang Tan (founder, Razer), Dato' Seri Dr. Wee Ka Siong (Malaysian Minister), Olivier Lim (former CEO, DBS)
- **Academia/Science:** Wang Gungwu (historian), Tommy Koh (diplomat), Kishore Mahbubani (diplomat/academic)

**Unique Identity:**
NUS is Singapore's **flagship comprehensive research university** — the oldest, largest, and highest-ranked. It offers the widest range of programmes (from Medicine to Music) and is consistently Asia's top-ranked university in QS. Its identity is built on research excellence, global partnerships (300+ exchange partners), and a strong liberal arts tradition via NUS College.

### B. Academic Programmes

**Undergraduate Degrees:**
- **Bachelor's with Honours (B.A.(Hons), B.Sc.(Hons), B.Eng.(Hons), LL.B.(Hons), etc.):** Typically 4 years direct honours. Some programmes are 3 years (general degree) + 1 year optional honours.
- **Modular system:** Students read modules (courses) worth modular credits (MCs). Most programmes require 160–180 MCs for honours (approx. 40–45 courses).
- **Double Degrees, Double Majors, Majors/Minors:** Widely available across faculties.

**Flagship Programmes:**
- **Yong Loo Lin School of Medicine:** 5-year MBBS, one of the most competitive programmes in Singapore (~20% acceptance rate, requires BMAT/UCAT interview).
- **Faculty of Law:** 4-year LL.B. and Graduate LL.B. (JD). Highly competitive — top 1% of A-Level scorers typically admitted.
- **School of Computing:** B.Comp. in Computer Science, Information Systems, Computer Engineering. Among the highest-demand programmes with booming graduate salaries.
- **Faculty of Engineering (via CDE):** B.Eng. in 10 specialisations (Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, Chemical, Biomedical, etc.). Most undergraduate engineering intake in Singapore.
- **NUS Business School:** BBA (Accountancy, Finance, Marketing, etc.), ranked among top 15 in QS subject rankings.

**Special Programmes:**
- **NUS College (NUSC):** The university's liberal arts honours college, launched AY2022/23, replacing both the University Scholars Programme (USP, est. 1999) and Yale-NUS College (2011–2025). NUSC students live in Cinnamon College (UTown) and take interdisciplinary seminars alongside their major. ~300 students per cohort.
- **Ridge View Residential College (RVRC):** A 2-year living-learning programme with a multidisciplinary curriculum.
- **Double Degree Programmes (DDP):** Many combos — Computer Science + Business, Engineering + Business, Law + anything, Medicine + PhD (MD-PhD).
- **Joint/Concurrent Degrees:** With Sciences Po (Paris), University of North Carolina (Chapel Hill), Peking University, and others.
- **Global Merit Scholarship / President's Scholarship:** Top-tier scholarships covering full fees, living allowance, and overseas exposure.

**Postgraduate Programmes:**
- **Masters by Coursework:** MBA (NUS Business School), Master of Computing, Master of Science (various), Master of Laws (LL.M.), Master of Public Policy, Master of Architecture, etc. 1–2 years.
- **Masters by Research (M.Eng., M.Sc., M.A.):** 1–2 years with thesis.
- **PhD:** Typically 4–5 years, full-time, with research scholarship (S$2,200–$3,500/month stipend).
- **Graduate Certificates/Diplomas:** Via NUS ISS (Institute of Systems Science) for working professionals.

### C. Admissions

**A-Level Entry Requirements (Indicative):**
- **Medicine:** AAA/A — 90 RP (10th percentile) — requires BMAT/UCAT, multiple mini-interviews (MMI)
- **Law (LL.B.):** AAA/A — 90 RP (10th percentile) — requires interview and writing test
- **Dentistry:** AAA/A — approx. 87.5 RP — requires interview and dexterity test
- **Computer Science:** AAA/A — 87.5–90 RP
- **Engineering:** AAB/B — 77.5–85 RP (depending on specialisation)
- **Business (BBA):** AAA/B — 85–87.5 RP
- **Arts & Social Sciences:** ABB/B — 73.75–80 RP
- **Science:** AAB/B — 78.75–85 RP
- **NUS College:** 90 RP typically (highly selective, ~4% acceptance)

_Note: RP = Rank Points for A-Level (90 max for 3 H2 + 1 H1 + GP + PW)._

**Polytechnic Diploma Entry (Indicative GPA cut-offs, 10th percentile):**
- **Medicine:** 3.85+ (almost exclusively JC pathway for Medicine)
- **Law:** 3.85+
- **Computer Science:** 3.80–3.90
- **Business:** 3.70–3.85
- **Engineering:** 3.60–3.80
- **Arts & Social Sciences:** 3.50–3.70
- **Science:** 3.60–3.80

**International Qualifications:**
- **IB Diploma:** Typically 39–43 points (Medicine requires 43+, Law 42+)
- **NUS High School Diploma:** Minimum GPA 4.5/5.0 for competitive courses
- **International A-Levels:** A*AA–AAA typical for competitive programmes

**Aptitude-Based Admissions (ABA):**
- NUS ABA allows up to **10% discretion** per course for applicants who do not meet IGP but demonstrate exceptional talent, leadership, or contributions. Requires strong portfolio, testimonials, and interview.

**International Student Admission:**
- Via separate International Qualifications (IQ) track
- Higher academic standards expected (typically equivalent to top 1–5% of cohort)
- IELTS/TOEFL required for non-English medium
- Applications through NUS portal

**Application Timeline:**
- **Applications open:** Early November (preceding year)
- **Applications close:** Late February (early March for Medicine/Law/Dentistry)
- **Admissions interviews:** March–April (Medicine, Law, Dentistry, Architecture, Nursing, Music)
- **Offer release:** Late March–May (rolling)
- **Acceptance deadline:** Late May (joint acceptance exercise with MOE)
- **Intake:** August (Semester 1 start)

**Acceptance Rates:**
NUS has an overall acceptance rate of approximately **5–10%** for international students, and about **15–25%** for local applicants depending on course. Medicine and Law typically admit fewer than 300 students each annually from 2,000+ applicants.

**MOE Tuition Grant Bond:**
- **Singapore Citizens:** No bond required (subsidy is a right)
- **Singapore PRs:** 3-year bond to work in a Singapore entity after graduation
- **International Students:** 3-year bond upon taking the Tuition Grant

**Financial Assistance Schemes:**
- **MOE Bursary:** Means-tested (household per-capita income ≤ S$2,700/month). Covers 15–100% of fees. Up to S$4,100/year for full-time undergraduates.
- **NUS Bursary:** Additional top-up for needy students (up to S$10,000/year).
- **CPF Education Scheme:** Use CPF savings to pay tuition (for SG citizens/PRs).
- **Tuition Fee Loan (TFL):** Up to 90% of subsidised fees from DBS/OCBC/UOB.
- **Study Loan:** Up to S$3,600/year for living expenses.
- **NUS Student Assistance Fund:** Emergency financial aid.

**Scholarships:**
- **NUS Global Merit Scholarship:** Full fees + S$6,000 living allowance + S$5,000 annual stipend + guaranteed overseas exchange. Bond-free.
- **NUS Merit Scholarship:** Full fees + S$2,000 living allowance.
- **NUS Sports & Performing Arts Scholarships:** For talented athletes/artists.
- **President's Scholarship:** Singapore's most prestigious publicly-awarded scholarship.
- **Various bonded scholarships:** **MOE Teaching Scholarship** (for teaching career), **Healthcare Merit Award** (MOH Holdings), **Public Service Commission (PSC) Scholarship** (for civil service).

### E. Campus Life & Student Experience

**Accommodation:**
- **Residential Colleges (RCs):** Cinnamon College (NUSC), Tembusu College, College of Alice & Peter Tan (CAPT), Ridge View RC. These offer integrated living-learning with academic programmes, dining halls, and faculty engagement. ~S$5,000–$7,000/year.
- **Hall of Residence:** 6 halls (Eusoff, Kent Ridge, King Edward VII, Raffles, Sheares, Temasek) with CCA-driven culture and hall spirit. ~S$3,000–$4,500/year.
- **UTown Residences:** Graduate student housing and some undergrad apartments.
- **Prince George's Park (PGP):** Apartment-style housing, popular with international students.
- **Off-campus:** Rental near Kent Ridge (S$600–$1,500/month for room in HDB or condo).

**Student Activities:**
- **200+ CCAs** covering sports, arts, community service, academic clubs, cultural groups
- **NUS Students' Union (NUSSU)** — central student government
- **Rag & Flag:** Annual charity fundraising campaign organised by freshmen
- **NUS Arts Festival** — annual showcase of student performing arts
- **Inter-Hall Games (IHG)** — intense sports rivalry between the 6 residential halls

**Exchange Programmes:**
- **Student Exchange Programme (SEP):** 300+ partner universities across 40+ countries. Semester or year-long.
- **NUS Overseas Colleges (NOC):** Flagship entrepreneurial internship programme — students intern at startups in Silicon Valley, New York, Beijing, Stockholm, Singapore, and other innovation hubs while taking entrepreneurship courses. One of the most distinctive programmes at NUS.
- **Double Degree/Joint Degree** with Sciences Po, UNC Chapel Hill, Peking University.

**Campus Facilities:**
- **Libraries:** 8 libraries (Central, C.J. Koh Law, Hon Sui Sen (Business), Medicine+Science, Music, East Asian, etc.)
- **Sports:** Multi-purpose sports complex at Kent Ridge (gym, swimming pool, track, tennis/basketball courts, climbing wall), UTown sports facilities
- **Innovation & Research:** NUS Enterprise (block71), Centre for Advanced 2D Materials, Institute for Health Innovation & Technology (iHealthtech), Solar Energy Research Institute (SERIS)
- **Student Wellness:** University Health Centre, counselling services, peer support networks

## 3. Nanyang Technological University (NTU)

### A. Profile & History

**History:**
NTU was established in **1981 as the Nanyang Technological Institute (NTI)** to train engineers for Singapore's industrialisation, taking over the former Nanyang University campus. In **1991**, NTI became **Nanyang Technological University (NTU)** through a merger with the National Institute of Education (NIE). It has since grown into Singapore's second-largest comprehensive university.

**Campus:**
- **Yunnan Garden Campus (Jurong West):** 200 hectares — Singapore's largest university campus. Known for its lush greenery, architecture (award-winning The Hive, Gaia, The Arc), and extensive facilities.
- **Novena Campus:** Lee Kong Chian School of Medicine (LKCMedicine), shared teaching facilities with Imperial College London.
- **One-North Campus:** NTU@one-north (satellite research space).

**Academic Structure:**
- **College of Engineering:** 6 schools (Civil & Environmental, Electrical & Electronic, Mechanical & Aerospace, Computer Science & Engineering, Materials Science & Engineering, Chemical & Biomedical)
- **College of Science:** 2 schools (Biological Sciences, Physical & Mathematical Sciences, Asian School of the Environment)
- **College of Humanities, Arts & Social Sciences:** 4 schools (Humanities, Art/Design/Media, Social Sciences, Nanyang Business School)
- **College of Professional Studies:** National Institute of Education (NIE), Lee Kong Chian School of Medicine (LKCMedicine), S. Rajaratnam School of International Studies (RSIS)
- **Graduate College:** NTU Graduate College oversees all graduate programmes.

**Student Population:**
- **Undergraduate:** ~25,000
- **Graduate:** ~8,000 (research + coursework)
- **Total:** ~33,000

**Rankings (2025):**
- **QS World University Rankings 2025:** #15
- **THE World University Rankings 2025:** #30
- **ARWU 2024:** #90
- **QS Subject Rankings 2025 highlights:**
  - Materials Science: #6
  - Engineering (Civil/Structural): #11
  - Engineering (Electrical/Electronic): #10
  - Communication & Media Studies: #13
  - Education (NIE): #11
  - Chemistry: #16
  - Computer Science: #27
- **QS WUR by Subject — Engineering & Technology:** #12 globally

**Notable Alumni:**
- **Business:** Ho Kwon Ping (founder, Banyan Tree Holdings), Tan Chong Huat (CEO, City Developments)
- **Arts/Media:** Jack Neo (comedian/filmmaker), Gurmit Singh (actor)
- **Politics:** Low Yen Ling (Minister of State), Desmond Lee (Minister for National Development)
- **Sports:** Joseph Schooling (Olympic gold medallist, swimmer)
- **Academia:** Subra Suresh (former President, Carnegie Mellon)
- **Education:** Oon Seng Tan (NIE Director), many Singapore school principals

**Unique Identity:**
NTU is Singapore's **specialist science & technology university** with a strong engineering tradition, but has become comprehensive with humanities, business, and medicine. Its campus is a living laboratory for sustainability (100% green-certified buildings, largest electric vehicle fleet in Singapore). Known for materials science (top 10 globally) and education via NIE — Singapore's sole teacher-training institution.

### B. Academic Programmes

**Undergraduate Degrees:**
- **Bachelor of Engineering (B.Eng.):** 4-year direct honours
- **Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.):** 4 years
- **Bachelor of Business (B.Bus.):** 4 years
- **Bachelor of Arts (B.A.):** 4 years
- **Bachelor of Social Sciences (B.Soc.Sci.):** 4 years
- **Bachelor of Medicine & Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS):** 5 years (joint with Imperial College London)
- **Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.):** 4 years (via NIE)
- **Bachelor of Science (Education) / Arts (Education):** 4 years (via NIE)

**Flagship Programmes:**
- **Engineering:** 6 schools under College of Engineering — largest engineering faculty in Singapore. Strong in Materials Science (#6 QS), Electrical/Electronic (#10 QS), Civil/Structural (#11 QS).
- **Materials Science:** NTU's #1 globally-ranked subject area. Pioneering work in 2D materials, biomaterials, and energy materials.
- **Lee Kong Chian School of Medicine (LKCMedicine):** Joint MBBS with Imperial College London, 5-year programme, heavy emphasis on technology-enhanced learning and early clinical exposure. ~100 students per cohort.
- **Education (NIE):** Singapore's sole teacher training institute — all school teachers (primary and secondary) are trained at NIE Nanyang Business School (NBS): Among top 40 business schools globally (FT rankings). Triple-crown accredited (AACSB, EQUIS, AMBA).

**Special Programmes:**
- **CN Yang Scholars Programme:** Premier science & engineering programme named after Nobel Laureate Chen Ning Yang. Full scholarship, overseas exchange, research attachment, mentoring by top faculty. ~30 students per cohort.
- **Renaissance Engineering Programme (REP):** A 4.5-year integrated Bachelor's + Master's programme designed to produce "Renaissance engineers" with both technical depth and business/humanities breadth. Full scholarship. ~40 students per cohort.
- **University Scholars Programme (USP @ NTU):** Interdisciplinary curriculum, research thesis, overseas exchange. Not to be confused with NUS USP.
- **NTU-NIE Teaching Scholars Programme:** For aspiring teachers with strong academic record.
- **Double Major Programmes:** Economics & Media, Psychology & Biological Sciences, etc.
- **NTU Premier Scholars Programme:** Umbrella for CN Yang, REP, USP, and NTU's undergraduate research experience.

**Postgraduate Programmes:**
- **Masters by Coursework:** MBA (Nanyang Fellows, Nanyang Executive MBA), Master of Science in various engineering fields, Master of Education, Master of Public Administration (via RSIS).
- **Masters by Research (M.Eng.):** 1–2 years.
- **PhD:** 4–5 years. Nanyang President's Graduate Scholarship (NPGS) is highly prestigious.

### E. Campus Life & Student Experience

**Accommodation:**
- **Residential Halls:** 23 Halls of Residence (Halls 1–16, Halls of Residence 2–9). Hall culture is strong — each hall has its own CCA groups, sports teams, and hall spirit. Single/double rooms. ~S$3,500–$5,000/year.
- **The Hive (Learning Hub):** Iconic 8-storey tower with 56 smart classrooms, opened 2015. Not accommodation but the centrepiece of campus.
- **Gaia (Pioneer Crescent):** Newest residential hall (2023), with 768 ensuite rooms and a strong sustainability design.
- **Graduate Halls:** Nanyang Grove, Graduate Residence at North Hill.
- **Off-campus:** Jurong West/Pioneer area — S$500–S$1,200/month for room rental.

**Student Activities:**
- **NTU Students' Union (NTUSU)** — central student government
- **70+ sports CCAs** with Hall competitions and inter-varsity meets
- **NTU Arts & Culture:** 30+ performing arts groups (choir, orchestra, dance, drama)
- **Hall culture:** Each hall has distinctive identity, annual Hall Fests and Inter-Hall Games
- **NTU Entrepreneurship Academy:** Supports student startup incubators
- **Volunteerism:** NTU Earthlink (environmental), Habitat for Humanity, community service projects

## 4. Singapore Management University (SMU)

SMU introduced the American-style seminar-based pedagogy to Singapore — small class sizes, interactive teaching, and a "Learn by Doing" philosophy.

- **Bras Basah Campus (City):** Located in the civic district, within the Bras Basah.Bugis arts and heritage precinct. SMU is an urban campus with no perimeter fence — its buildings (six schools) are integrated with the city. Campus comprises 6 interconnected buildings: School of Accountancy, Lee Kong Chian School of Business, School of Economics, School of Information Systems, School of Law, College of Graduate Research Studies.

## 5. Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD)

### A. Profile & History

**History:**
SUTD was founded in **2009** in partnership with the **Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)** as Singapore's fourth autonomous university, with a unique focus on technology and design. It received its first cohort of students in 2012. The MIT partnership (which ended its formal phase in 2020 but continues in spirit) shaped SUTD's curriculum — heavily project-based, design-centric, and interdisciplinary.

**Campus:**
- **Changi Campus (SUTD @ Changi):** Located at Upper Changi Road East, next to Somapah Road (near Expo MRT). The campus comprises two buildings: the main academic building with state-of-the-art design labs, maker spaces, and studios.
- **Size:** ~35,000 sqm (compact but highly integrated campus).
- A new **permanent campus** at the same location is under development to triple capacity.
- **Changi City:** Proximity to Changi Business Park, Singapore Expo, Changi Airport.

**Academic Structure (4 Pillars — SUTD calls them 'Pillars' instead of Schools):**
- **Engineering Product Development (EPD)**
- **Engineering Systems & Design (ESD)**
- **Architecture & Sustainable Design (ASD)**
- **Design & Artificial Intelligence (DAI)** — launched 2023, replacing Information Systems Technology & Design (ISTD)
- **SUTD Academy** (executive education)
- **SUTD Graduate College**

**Student Population:**
- **Undergraduate:** ~1,500
- **Graduate:** ~500
- **Total:** ~2,000

**Rankings:**
- **QS World University Rankings 2025:** #461
- **THE World University Rankings 2025:** #351–400

- Note: SUTD is small and young; rankings have been improving steadily.

**Notable Alumni:**

**Unique Identity:**
SUTD is Singapore's **design & technology university** — the only one with a fully interdisciplinary, project-based curriculum. Its defining features:

- **Freshmore year:** All students take the same common curriculum — Mathematics, Sciences, Humanities, and Design — before specialising.

## 7. Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS)

### A. Profile & History

**History:**
SUSS began as the **Singapore Institute of Management (SIM) Open University Centre** in 2004, before becoming **UniSIM** (SIM University) in 2005 — Singapore's first private university offering part-time degrees for working adults. In **2017**, UniSIM was restructured as the **Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS)** — Singapore's sixth autonomous university, with a renewed mission focused on social sciences and lifelong learning.

**Campus:**
- **Clementi Campus:** Located at 461 Clementi Road, near the junction of Clementi Road and Commonwealth Avenue West. The campus comprises:
  - Main Block (Block A–E) — classrooms, lecture theatres, library, faculty offices
  - SUSS Centre for Experiential Learning
  - Future campus expansion: SUSS is developing a new campus at the existing site with additional facilities

**Academic Structure (6 Schools):**
- **School of Humanities & Behavioural Sciences (SHBS)**
- **School of Business (SBIZ)**
- **School of Law (SOL)**
- **School of Science & Technology (SST)**
- **Graduate School**
- **SUSS Academy** (continuing education & lifelong learning)

**Student Population:**
- **Undergraduate:** ~15,000 (includes ~8,000 full-time, ~7,000 part-time)
- **Graduate:** ~1,000
- **Total:** ~16,000

**Rankings:**
- **Not ranked** in QS, THE, or ARWU world university rankings (SUSS does not participate in these ranking exercises, as its mission differs from research-intensive universities).
- **Applied research focus:** SUSS focuses on applied social research rather than basic research.
- **Graduate employability:** Assessed via GES independently by MOE.

**Notable Alumni:**
- SUSS has a strong alumni base in the social service sector, early childhood education, HR, counselling, and legal fields.
- As a young autonomous university (2017), its notable alumni are largely drawn from its UniSIM days and include professionals in HR, social work, early childhood education, and financial services.

**Unique Identity:**
SUSS is Singapore's **university of social sciences** with a tripartite mission:
- **Social sciences education:** Programmes focused on human services, community work, psychology, sociology, and related fields.
- **Lifelong learning:** Largest provider of part-time degree programmes in Singapore. Many students are working adults upgrading their qualifications.
- **Applied research:** Research with direct community impact.
- **Campus for all ages:** SUSS pioneered Singapore's SkillsFuture movement — many programmes are stackable credentials (certificates → diplomas → degrees) for mid-career workers.

## 8. University of the Arts Singapore (UAS)

### A. Profile & History

**History:**
UAS is Singapore's **first and only publicly-funded arts university**, launched in **2024** through the merger of two established private arts institutions:
- **LASALLE College of the Arts** — founded 1984 by the De La Salle Brothers. Known for contemporary arts education, fine arts, design, and performing arts.
- **NAFA (Nanyang Academy of Fine Arts)** — founded 1938. Singapore's oldest arts institution, known for traditional Chinese arts, music, dance, and visual arts.

Together, they form the **University of the Arts Singapore (UAS)** — a single autonomous university with two colleges (LASALLE and NAFA) retaining their distinct identities.

**Campuses:**
- **LASALLE Campus (McNally Street):** City campus in the Bras Basah arts district. State-of-the-art facilities including:
  - Institute of Contemporary Arts (ICA)
  - Performance theatres, black box studios
  - Design studios, media labs, gallery spaces
- **NAFA Campus (Bendeemer Road):** Near Little India. Historic campus with:
  - Lee Foundation Theatre
  - Art galleries, music practice rooms
  - Dance studios, art studios

**Academic Structure:**
- **UAS-LASALLE:** Faculty of Fine Arts, Faculty of Design, Faculty of Media & Communication, Faculty of Performing Arts
- **UAS-NAFA:** School of Art & Design, School of Music, School of Dance & Theatre, School of Arts Management
- Central **UAS Senate and administration** oversees both colleges

**Student Population:**
- **LASALLE:** ~2,500 students
- **NAFA:** ~3,000 students
- **Total UAS:** ~5,500 students

**Rankings:**
- **Not yet ranked** as a university (UAS launched 2024). However, its constituent institutions are well-regarded:
  - LASALLE featured in QS Art & Design rankings (#151–200 range)
  - NAFA is regionally recognised for traditional arts and music
- UAS is expected to enter rankings in future cycles

**Notable Alumni:**
- **LASALLE:** Syafiq Halid (contemporary artist), several award-winning filmmakers and designers
- **NAFA:** Dick Lee (renowned musician/composer), Cultural Medallion recipients (Singapore's highest arts honour)
- Graduates work at Singapore Art Museum, National Gallery Singapore, Esplanade, Singapore Symphony Orchestra, and in creative industries across film, fashion, gaming, and advertising

**Unique Identity:**
UAS is Singapore's **national arts university** — the first publicly-funded institution dedicated entirely to the arts. Its identity is built on:
- **Dual-college structure:** LASALLE (contemporary, global arts) + NAFA (traditional, heritage arts with strong Asian focus)
- **Creative ecosystem:** Integrated with the Bras Basah arts and heritage district (Singapore Art Museum, National Museum, Esplanade, School of the Arts)
- **Industry-first approach:** Strong ties with Creative Industries Singapore (CIS), Singapore Tourism Board, and arts organisations
- **Complementary strengths:** LASALLE excels in contemporary arts, film, design, and performing arts; NAFA excels in traditional Chinese/Asian arts, music, dance, and arts education

### E. Campus Life & Student Experience

**Accommodation:**
- **No dedicated on-campus housing.** Both LASALLE and NAFA are city campuses.
- Students rent off-campus in the Bras Basah, Little India, Bugis, or Lavender areas.
- Some students live at **independent hostels** near LASALLE (e.g., Airbnb, student hostels).

**Student Activities:**
- **UAS Student Board** — joint student council across both colleges
- **60+ CCAs** across both campuses — focus on artistic practice, open studios, jam sessions
- **LASALLE Show:** Annual graduate showcase — one of Singapore's most important arts events
- **NAFA Gradshow:** Annual exhibition and performance showcase
- **Inter-College Arts Festival:** Joint LASALLE-NAFA arts festival (new with UAS)
- **First Fridays:** Open studio sessions where students showcase works-in-progress

**Exchange Programmes:**
- **UAS Global Exchange:** 40+ partner arts institutions worldwide (Royal College of Art, Parsons New York, Central Saint Martins London, Beijing Film Academy, Tokyo University of the Arts)
- **Artist-in-Residence:** Many programmes have embedded residency components
- **Summer Arts Programme:** International summer school at partner universities

**Internships:**
- **Arts Internships:** Embedded in every programme — typically 8–16 weeks at arts organisations, galleries, production studios, design agencies
- **LASALLE Industry Partners:** Singapore Art Museum, National Gallery, Esplanade, Mediacorp, Ogilvy, BBDO, and many design studios
- **NAFA Industry Partners:** Singapore Symphony Orchestra, Esplanade, Chinese Development Assistance Council (CDAC), various arts education centres

**Campus Facilities:**
- **LASALLE:**
  - Institute of Contemporary Arts (ICA) gallery
  - Black box theatres, dance studios, music recording studios
  - Film sound stages and post-production suites
  - Digital media labs, 3D printing lab
  - **LASALLE Library:** Strong collection in contemporary art, design, film
- **NAFA:**
  - Lee Foundation Theatre (500-seat)
  - Art galleries (NAFA Gallery, Lim Hak Tai Gallery)
  - Music performance halls, practice rooms (piano labs, ensemble rooms)
  - Dance studios with sprung floors
  - **NAFA Library:** Strong in Asian art history, traditional Chinese arts
