## 1. Overview

The Singapore Government issues debt instruments through the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) to raise funds and develop the domestic bond market. Three main categories serve different investor needs:

| Instrument | Tenor | Best For |
|:-----------|:------|:---------|
| **SGS Bonds** | 2–30 years | Long-term income, portfolio diversification |
| **T-bills** | 6–12 months | Cash management, short-term parking |
| **SSB** | 10 years (step-up, redeemable anytime) | Flexible savings, emergency fund, regular savers |

All three are backed by the Republic of Singapore, rated **AAA by S&P, Moody's, and Fitch** — among the highest sovereign ratings globally. Interest income from all three is **tax-exempt** for individual Singapore residents. No capital gains tax applies.

## 2. SGS Bonds (Singapore Government Securities Bonds)

### 2.1 What Are SGS Bonds?

Medium- to long-term debt instruments that form the backbone of Singapore's domestic bond market. They serve as benchmark yield references for the entire SGD fixed-income market.

- **Tenors:** 2, 5, 10, 15, 20, 30 years (benchmark issues). A 50-year bond also exists.
- **Denomination:** S$1,000 per unit
- **Coupon:** Fixed-rate interest paid semi-annually (every 6 months)
- **Issuance:** By auction — as **new issues** (establishing a new benchmark) or **reopenings** (adding to an existing benchmark bond at current market prices)
- **Secondary market:** Traded OTC through MAS-licensed Primary Dealers
- **Credit rating:** AAA (S&P, Moody's, Fitch)

### 2.2 How SGS Bond Auctions Work

SGS bonds use a uniform-price auction. Understanding the mechanics is essential.

**Competitive vs Non-Competitive Bidding:**
- **Non-competitive bid** (recommended for individuals): You accept the cut-off yield determined by the auction. Non-competitive bids are always filled first, up to **40% of total issuance**.
- **Competitive bid** (for institutions/experienced investors): You specify the minimum yield you are willing to accept. Bids are ranked from lowest to highest yield. The cut-off yield is the highest yield at which MAS can fill the total issuance.

**Pricing:**
- All successful bidders pay the **same cut-off price** (uniform-price auction)
- The cut-off price = present value of all future cash flows discounted at the cut-off yield
- For reopenings, accrued interest from the last coupon date is added

**Auction Calendar:**
- MAS publishes the calendar on mas.gov.sg quarterly
- Announcements typically 1–2 weeks before each auction
- 2-year bonds roughly every 3 months; 5-year and 10-year quarterly; longer tenors semi-annually

**Minimums:**
- Non-competitive: Min S$1,000, max S$2M per auction per individual
- Competitive individuals: Min S$1,000
- Primary Dealers are obligated to participate and maintain two-way markets

**New Issues vs Reopenings:**
- **New issue:** Fresh bond with a new coupon rate and maturity date
- **Reopening:** Additional tranche of an existing bond — same coupon and maturity, but price adjusts for current market yields and accrued interest

### 2.3 How to Buy SGS Bonds

1. Have a **CDP account** linked to DBS/POSB, OCBC, or UOB
2. During the application window (~5–7 business days before auction):
   - Use ATM: Insert card → Investments → SGS Bonds → Non-competitive bid → Enter S$1,000+ → Confirm S$2 fee
   - Or internet banking: Invest → Bonds → SGS → Select issue → Enter amount
3. Funds are debited on application; returned if unallotted
4. Fees: **S$2 per application** (ATM/online). Waived for some CPF/SRS applications.

### 2.4 Returns & Taxation

**Coupon Yield vs Yield-to-Maturity (YTM):**
- **Coupon yield** = Annual coupon / Face value × 100%. Fixed for the bond's life.
- **YTM** = Total return if held to maturity, accounting for purchase price (discount/premium), coupons, and reinvestment. The cut-off yield IS the YTM.
- Example: 2-year bond with 2.875% coupon purchased at 103.663 has a YTM of 1.60% — the premium paid reduces effective yield below the coupon.

**Tax:** Interest is **tax-exempt** for individual Singapore tax residents. No capital gains tax. No withholding tax for residents. Non-residents may face 15% withholding (reduced under tax treaties).

### 2.5 Risks

- **Interest rate risk (primary):** Bond prices fall when yields rise. Longer tenors = greater price sensitivity (duration risk). A 10-year bond bought at 2.09% could lose 5–10% in market value if yields rise 1%. However, held to maturity you receive full face value + all coupons.
- **Reinvestment risk:** Coupon payments must be reinvested at prevailing rates (may be lower).
- **Inflation risk:** Fixed coupons lose purchasing power if inflation accelerates.
- **Credit risk:** Near-zero (AAA sovereign, never defaulted).
- **Liquidity:** Generally good for benchmark issues; thinner for older reopened tranches.

## 3. Treasury Bills (T-bills)

### 3.1 What Are T-bills?

Short-term government debt — the most popular short-term investment vehicle in Singapore.

- **Tenors:** 6-month (26-week) and 1-year (52-week) standard. 1-month bills issued occasionally.
- **Denomination:** S$1,000
- **Structure:** Zero-coupon — issued at a discount to face value, matures at par (S$100 per S$100 face)
- **No periodic coupon payments** — return is simply the discount earned at maturity

### 3.2 How T-bill Auctions Work

Same uniform-price auction model as SGS bonds.

**Cut-off yield & price:**
- Price = 100 / (1 + Yield × Days/365)
- Example: 6-month T-bill (182 days) at cut-off yield 1.50% → Price = 100 / (1 + 0.015 × 182/365) = **S$99.252 per S$100**

**Allocation Rules:**
1. Non-competitive bids filled first, up to 40% of total issuance
2. Remaining competitive bids filled from lowest to highest yield
3. At cut-off, allotments may be **pro-rated** if demand > supply
4. **Bid-to-cover ratio** = Total applications / Issuance amount. >2.0 indicates strong demand.

**Schedule:** 6-month T-bills every ~2 weeks; 1-year T-bills every ~6 months. Calendar on mas.gov.sg.

### 3.3 How to Buy T-bills

| Method | Details | Fee |
|:-------|:--------|:---:|
| DBS/OCBC/UOB ATM or internet banking | Most popular; min S$1,000 | S$2 |
| CPFIS (CPF Ordinary Account) | Min S$500; interest back to CPF OA | Waived at some banks |
| SRS (Supplementary Retirement Scheme) | Min S$1,000; tax-deferred | S$2 or waived |
| CDP account | Required for all holdings | — |

**Deadline:** Typically 2 business days before auction, 9 PM. Funds debited on application; refunded if unallotted.

### 3.5 Taxation

The discount is treated as interest income — **tax-exempt** for individual Singapore residents. No withholding tax for residents. Non-residents may face 15%.

### 3.6 Risks

- **Reinvestment risk (primary):** Short tenors mean frequent rollover at potentially lower rates — a key concern in a declining rate environment.
- **Inflation risk:** A 1.50% yield is a negative real return if CPI exceeds it.
- **Credit risk:** Near-zero (AAA government guarantee).
- **Opportunity cost:** Missing equity gains in bull markets.

## 4. Singapore Savings Bonds (SSB)

### 4.1 What Are SSB?

A retail-friendly government bond designed for individual investors, first issued in October 2015. Created to address the needs of the average saver seeking safety, flexibility, and simplicity.

**Key innovations:**
- **No penalty for early redemption** — redeem anytime after 6 months at par (no loss of principal)
- **Step-up interest rates** — rates increase yearly, rewarding longer holding periods
- **Retail-focused** — low minimum, free to apply, simple process

**Key Features:**
- **Tenor:** 10 years (but redeemable anytime after 6 months)
- **Denomination:** S$500
- **Maximum holding:** S$200,000 per individual across all series
- **Non-transferable:** Cannot be sold — only redeemed back to the government
- **Monthly issuance:** New series every month

### 4.2 How SSB Works

**Stepped coupon structure:** Pre-determined rates that increase step by step from Year 1 to Year 10:

| Year | Interest Rate (%) | Average Return to Date (%) |
|:----:|:-----------------:|:-------------------------:|
| 1 | 1.46 | 1.46 |
| 2 | 1.75 | 1.60 |
| 3 | 1.80 | 1.67 |
| 4 | 1.80 | 1.70 |
| 5 | 1.90 | 1.74 |
| 6 | 2.11 | 1.80 |
| 7 | 2.27 | 1.86 |
| 8 | 2.43 | 1.93 |
| 9 | 2.58 | 1.99 |
| 10 | 2.72 | **2.06** |

*SBAUG26 (July 2026). Invest S$10K and hold 10 years → total interest of S$2,081.*

The **10-year average return** is the headline metric. SSB interest rates are linked to the daily average SGS benchmark yields published by MAS in the preceding month.

**Interest payment:** Semi-annually into your designated bank account.

**Early redemption:**
- After 6 months, redeem any amount (partial or full) at par — always get back principal in full
- Accrued interest included
- Processed in ~2 weeks via ATM or internet banking
- **No penalty, no market price risk** — this is SSB's defining advantage

**Monthly issuance & allocation:**
- Application period: 1st to ~25th of each month
- Current offer size: S$300M per issue
- If oversubscribed, pro-rated allotment (common in 2022–2023 when 10-year returns hit 3.5%)
- Recent 2026 issues under-subscribed (S$202M–S$244M vs S$300M offered)

### 4.3 How to Buy SSB

- **Free to apply** — no S$2 fee
- CDP account required (free to open — see Section 7)
- Apply via DBS/OCBC/UOB internet banking or ATMs
- Minimum S$500, in S$500 increments
- **CPFIS and SRS** can also be used
- Results: ~3rd business day of following month

### 4.5 Taxation

**Tax-exempt** for individual Singapore tax residents. No withholding tax.

### 4.6 SSB vs T-bills vs SGS Bonds — Key Differences

| Criterion | Winner | Why |
|:----------|:-------|:----|
| **Liquidity (early access)** | **SSB** | Redeem anytime at par. SGS must be sold at market price (potential loss). T-bills: hold to maturity. |
| **Yield (same tenor)** | **SGS bonds** | 10Y SGS benchmark (2.13%) > SSB 10Y avg (2.06%). Gap widens for longer tenors. |
| **Retail accessibility** | **SSB** | Free to apply, S$500 min, no auction mechanics to learn. |
| **Holding limit** | **T-bills/SGS** | No cap. SSB capped at S$200K/person. |
| **Minimum investment** | **SSB** | S$500 vs S$1,000 for T-bills and SGS bonds. |
| **Capital guarantee** | **SSB/T-bills** | SSB: par anytime. T-bills: par at maturity. SGS: only at maturity (market price risk before). |

## 6. Investment Strategy Recommendations

### 6.1 By Investment Horizon

| Horizon | Recommended | Rationale |
|:--------|:------------|:----------|
| 0–6 months | Savings account / money market fund | T-bills min 6M; SSB has 6M lock-up |
| 6–12 months | T-bills | Best short-term yield; simple, predictable |
| 1–3 years | SSB or T-bill ladder | SSB: flexibility to redeem. T-bill ladder: regular maturities. |
| 3–5 years | SSB (hold for avg returns) | Years 3–5 avg returns competitive; can redeem anytime |
| 5–10 years | SSB (full 10-year hold) | Maximises step-up benefit (years 6–10 rates highest) |
| 10+ years | SGS bonds (10Y+) | Higher yields than SSB for same tenor; portfolio diversification |

### 6.2 Laddering Strategy

A bond ladder spreads investments across maturities to manage reinvestment risk and provide regular liquidity.

**Example ladder (S$100,000):**
- **S$10,000** 6-month T-bill — rolling cash buffer
- **S$10,000** 1-year T-bill — short-term yield
- **S$30,000** SSB (3 series, staggered months) — emergency funds + flexibility
- **S$20,000** 5-year SGS bond — medium-term yield enhancement
- **S$30,000** 10-year SGS bond — long-term income + diversification

This provides monthly liquidity from T-bill maturities, full flexibility from SSB, and higher yields from SGS bonds for the longer-term portion.

### 6.3 CPF OA vs T-bills vs SSB

CPF OA earns a legislated **2.5% p.a.** (plus an extra 1% on the first S$60K under the Special/MediSave floor). As of July 2026, **all SGS instruments yield below CPF OA**:

- 6-month T-bill: 1.50% — below CPF OA
- SSB 10-year avg: 2.06% — below CPF OA
- 10-year SGS bond: 2.13% — below CPF OA
- 30-year SGS bond: 2.25% — below CPF OA

**Takeaway:** In the current low-rate environment, leaving funds in CPF OA provides a **higher risk-free return** than any SGS instrument. The exception: SSB offers **liquidity and flexibility** that CPF OA cannot match (CPF OA funds are largely locked for housing/education/retirement). Use CPF OA for T-bills/SSB only when yields exceed 2.5% (as they did in 2022–2023 when T-bills hit 3.5–4.0%).

### 6.4 SSB vs Fixed Deposits

Choose SSB over FD when:
1. You need **flexibility** — SSB redeems anytime with no penalty; FD charges early withdrawal penalties
2. You want **rising income** — SSB rates step up yearly; FD rates are flat throughout the term
3. You're investing for **>3 years** — SSB's 3-year avg return (1.74%) becomes competitive with 3-year FD rates
4. You want to build a **regular savings habit** — monthly SSB subscriptions work like a recurring deposit

Current comparison (July 2026): SSB Year 1 (1.46%) is below best 12-month FD (1.60%) but above best 3-month FD (1.35%).

### 6.5 T-bills vs High-Yield Savings Accounts

High-yield savings accounts (OCBC 360, UOB One, DBS Multiplier) offer 2.0–3.5% but require salary crediting, card spend, or other conditions. T-bills offer:
- **No conditions** — guaranteed cut-off yield regardless of banking activity
- **Rate locked** for 6–12 months
- **Simple** — no monthly criteria or tier calculations

T-bills win when simplicity matters. Savings accounts win if you can consistently meet criteria.

### 6.6 Dollar-Cost Averaging with SSB

1. Set a monthly budget (e.g., S$1,000)
2. Apply for each month's SSB issue (applications are free)
3. Build a ladder of SSB series at different average returns
4. When rates are high (like 2023), new issues lock in higher step-up rates
5. **Swap** older low-yielding SSB for newer high-yielding ones when profitable — use ilovessb.com/swap or Beansprout's swap calculator to check if swapping is worth the interest foregone during the transition

## 7. Application Process Step-by-Step

### 7.1 Opening a CDP Account

Free to open. Required for all SGS/SSB/T-bill holdings.
1. Go to sgx.com/cdp or apply via bank internet banking
2. Submit NRIC (Singaporeans/PRs) or Passport + Employment Pass (foreigners)
3. Provide recent proof of address (utility bill, bank statement — within 3 months)
4. Processing: **~5 business days**
5. Link to your DBS/POSB, OCBC, or UOB bank account
6. If you already have a CDP account for stock trading, the same account works

### 7.2 Setting Up Internet Banking

**DBS/POSB:** Login → Invest → Bonds → SGS/T-bills/SSB. Ensure CDP linked under My Profile.
**OCBC:** Login → Invest → Bonds. Link CDP under Settings.
**UOB:** Login → Invest → Securities → SGS/SSB. Ensure CDP registered.

### 7.3 Applying via ATM (SGS Bonds & T-bills)

Insert ATM card → PIN → Investments → Choose instrument → Select issue → Non-competitive bid → Enter amount (S$1,000+ in S$1K increments) → Confirm S$2 fee → Keep receipt.

### 7.4 Applying via Internet Banking (SSB & T-bills)

Login → Investments → Select instrument (SSB/T-bill/SGS) → Choose issue → Enter amount (SSB: S$500+, T-bill/SGS: S$1,000+) → Confirm. Funds debited immediately.

### 7.5 CPF and SRS Application

**CPFIS:** Activate CPFIS at DBS/OCBC/UOB → Login to CPFIS portal → Select T-bill or SSB → Enter CPF OA amount (min S$500 for SSB, S$500 for T-bills). No S$2 fee. Interest flows back to CPF OA.

**SRS:** Login to SRS account → Invest → SSB or T-bills → Enter SRS amount. Interest credited to SRS account (tax-deferred).

### 7.6 Checking Allotment Results

**T-bills/SGS bonds:** MAS website on auction day (by 1 PM). Bank SMS/email notification. CDP updated in 2–3 days.

**SSB:** MAS SSB website ~3rd business day of following month. Bank notification sent automatically.

### 7.7 Unallotted Funds

Refunded to bank account within **2–5 business days**. No interest paid on unallocated funds during the application period.

## Conclusion

Singapore Government Securities offer a spectrum of safe, tax-efficient investment options for every investor need:

- **T-bills** for short-term cash management (6–12 months) — simple and predictable, though current yields (1.36–1.50%) trail CPF OA (2.5%)
- **SGS bonds** for long-term income — higher yields (2-year: 1.61% to 30-year: 2.25%) but carry interest rate risk if sold before maturity
- **SSB** for flexible savings — penalty-free redemption and step-up rates, capped at S$200K per individual

In the current (July 2026) low-rate environment, all three yield below CPF OA, but SSB's liquidity and flexibility make it valuable for emergency funds. A **laddered approach** combining all three provides the optimal balance of liquidity, yield, and diversification.

SSB is particularly appealing for regular savers who value the ability to access their money penalty-free — a feature no fixed deposit or CPF OA can match. When rates eventually rise again, rolling T-bills and SSB into new issues will reward patient investors.
