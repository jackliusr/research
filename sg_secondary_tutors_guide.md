## Executive Summary

Singapore's private tutoring market is estimated at **$1.4 billion annually**, with secondary school students making up the largest segment. Over 60% of Singaporean secondary students receive some form of private tuition.

**Key findings:**
- **Typical rates:** $25-50/hr (undergrad tutor), $40-80/hr (graduate), $80-150/hr (MOE/NIE teacher)
- **Most popular agencies:** FamilyTutor, ChampionTutor, SmileTutor, TutorTime, Kiasu Tutors
- **No registration fees** — all major agencies charge $0 to sign up
- **Free trial lessons** — standard across most agencies
- **Tutor replacement** — typically free within first 2-3 lessons
- **Math and Science command 15-25% premium** over Humanities
- **Peak season (O-level prep, Feb-Nov):** Rates 20-30% higher

## 8. How to Choose a Good Tutor

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Define your needs**
- What subjects? Stream (Express/NA/NT/IP)?
- What's the problem — lack of understanding? Exam technique? Motivation?
- Budget per month?
- Home, centre, or online?

**Step 2: Choose your search method**

| Method | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Agency (FamilyTutor, ChampionTutor, etc.)** | Vetted tutors; replacement guarantee; free trial | Agency commission built into rate |
| **Direct freelance (Carousell, referrals)** | Lower rate (no commission) | No guarantee; self-vetting required |
| **Tuition centre 1-to-1 (Matrix Math, Toggle)** | Structured curriculum; centre resources | Less flexible location; may be pricier |
| **Online platform (Tutopiya)** | Recordings; flexible; wider tutor pool | Requires tech setup; less personal |

**Step 3: Shortlist 2-3 tutors**
- Through your chosen agency or platform
- Ask for the tutor's profile: education, experience, past student results

**Step 4: Take trial lessons**
- Most agencies offer a free or discounted first lesson
- Use it to assess: teaching style, rapport with your child, lesson preparation

**Step 5: Evaluate after 1 month**
- Has your child's confidence improved? (Often visible before grades)
- Does the tutor prepare lesson materials?
- Is communication with you (parent) adequate?

### What to Look For in a Tutor

| Green Flags | Red Flags |
|------------|-----------|
| Prepares lesson materials in advance | Shows up unprepared; "just do worksheets" |
| Communicates with parents after lessons | No parent update after weeks of lessons |
| Can explain concepts in multiple ways | Only teaches one method; can't adapt |
| Knows the MOE syllabus and exam format | Doesn't know latest syllabus changes |
| Has a clear plan and learning goals | No structure; each lesson is ad-hoc |
| Builds rapport with the student | Student dreads tutor sessions |

### Questions to Ask the Agency

1. "Can you share the tutor's past O-Level results for this subject?"
2. "How do you handle a replacement if it doesn't work out?"
3. "Is the trial lesson free or discounted?"
4. "What's the cancellation policy?"
5. "Does the tutor have experience with my child's stream/school?"

## 9. Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why It's a Problem | How to Avoid |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Hiring a tutor too late (Sec 4 mid-year)** | O-Level prep is most effective over 1-2 years, not 6 months | Start in Sec 3 or early Sec 4 |
| **Over-tutoring (5+ subjects)** | Burnout; no time for self-study | Limit to 2-3 core subjects |
| **Expecting instant results** | Grades take 3-6 months to show improvement | Commit for at least one school term |
| **Cheapest option first** | Undergrad tutors may lack experience for exam-level prep | Match tutor quality to the need |
| **Ignoring student input** | If your child dislikes the tutor, learning won't happen | Involve child in selection process |
| **Switching tutors too often** | Each new tutor needs a ramp-up period | Give each tutor at least 2-3 months |
| **Using the same tutor for all subjects** | No one is equally strong in everything | Different tutors for different subjects |
| **Not checking syllabus changes** | O-Level syllabus evolves; outdated tutors may teach wrong content | Verify that tutor uses current MOE materials |
