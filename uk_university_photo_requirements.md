## 1. UCAS Application (Undergraduate)

### Key fact: UCAS does NOT require a photo

The **Universities and Colleges Admissions Service (UCAS)** handles applications for undergraduate programmes at UK universities. A common misconception — particularly among international students from countries whose application systems routinely include a photograph — is that UCAS requires a headshot. **This is incorrect.** UCAS does not ask applicants to upload, attach, or submit a photograph at any point in the standard undergraduate process.

### Why the misconception exists

- Many non-UK systems (e.g., US Common App, some Asian university portals) require a photo for identification.
- Students conflate the UCAS application with the later visa stage, where a photo is required.
- Some third-party tools (e.g., PassportPic.ai) market "UCAS profile photos" — these are not official UCAS requirements.

### What UCAS actually requires

| Section | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| **Personal details** | Name, address, date of birth, nationality, contact info |
| **Course choices** | Up to five course selections |
| **Education history** | Schools, qualifications obtained and predicted, with grades |
| **Employment history** | Up to five paid jobs with details |
| **Personal statement** | 4,000-character essay on motivation for chosen course |
| **Reference** | Academic reference from teacher or adviser |
| **Fee payment** | Application fee |

### Exception: Course-specific photo requests

While UCAS itself collects no photos, some courses or universities may separately request photographs:
- **Art, design, architecture:** Portfolio submission may include a photo for interview identification.
- **Drama, performance:** Audition registration sometimes requires an ID photo.
- **Post-application identity checks:** Some universities email applicants directly requesting a passport-style photo for internal records ahead of enrolment.

These requests are handled **outside** the UCAS system, usually via the university's own applicant portal.

## 2. UK Student Visa (Student Route / Tier 4)

This is the stage where photo requirements become critical. The Student Route visa requires a digital photograph as part of the online application and a separate in-person biometric photograph at the Visa Application Centre (VAC).

### 2.1 Online visa application — digital photo upload

When completing the application on GOV.UK, applicants must upload a digital passport-style photograph meeting UKVI specifications.

#### Size & dimensions

- **45 mm high × 35 mm wide** (portrait orientation — UK standard).
- **Note:** This is different from the Singapore passport photo size (35 mm × 45 mm, landscape). The UK format is taller than it is wide; the Singapore format is wider than it is tall.

#### Background colour

- **Plain light grey or cream** — specific to UK visa applications.
- **Not white.** UK passport photos can have a white background; UK visa photos require light grey or cream. This is a common point of confusion.
- No patterns, gradients, or shadows on the background.

#### Expression & appearance

- **Neutral expression** — no frowning or smiling. **Mouth closed** — teeth must not be visible.
- **Eyes open and clearly visible** — no hair covering them.
- **Face fully visible** — from crown of head to chin, centred, facing directly forward.
- **No shadows** on the face or behind the head.

#### Glasses

- **Not allowed.** Since the 2018 UKVI rule change, glasses must be removed for visa application photos, even prescription glasses. (Contact lenses are fine.)

#### Head coverings

- **Not permitted** except for religious or medical reasons. Even then, the full face must be visible from chin to crown, with no shadows cast by the covering.

#### Digital upload specifications

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| File format | JPEG or PNG |
| File size | Typically ≤ 1 MB (check portal; some accept up to 10 MB) |
| Resolution | Minimum 600 dpi recommended |
| Colour | Colour only |
| Freshness | Taken within the last **6 months** |
| Method | Smartphone, tablet, or digital camera acceptable |
| Editing | No filters, no editing that alters facial structure |

#### UKVI photo vs. UK passport photo — key differences

| Feature | UK Passport Photo | UK Visa Photo |
|---------|------------------|---------------|
| Background | White or off-white | Light grey or cream |
| Glasses | Allowed (if no glare, eyes visible) | **Not allowed** |
| Digital code | Can use photo code from shop | Must upload directly |
| Dimensions | 45 mm × 35 mm | 45 mm × 35 mm |

### 2.2 How the digital photo is submitted

- Uploaded during the online visa application form on GOV.UK.
- The portal may run an automated check to validate the photo.
- If the check fails, you will be prompted to upload a new one before proceeding.

## 3. Biometric Appointment

After submitting the online visa application, all Student Route applicants must attend an in-person **biometric appointment**.

### Where to go in Singapore

> **VFS Global UK Visa Application Centre**
> 135 Cecil Street, #08-01, Philippine Airlines Building
> Singapore 069536

Applications from Singapore are handled by **VFS Global**, the UK government's commercial partner.

### What happens at the appointment

1. **Photograph taken on-site** — This is the photo that appears on your visa vignette and was historically used for the BRP. Same standards as passport photos: 45 mm × 35 mm, neutral expression, plain background, no glasses, no head coverings (except religious).
2. **Digital fingerprints** — All ten fingerprints scanned electronically (inkless).
3. **Document verification** — Passport and supporting documents checked.

### Booking

- Booked online after submitting the visa application and paying the fee.
- Priority/premium appointments available at extra cost.
- Bring passport, appointment confirmation, and document checklist.

## 4. BRP (Biometric Residence Permit) & eVisa

### What is a BRP?

The **Biometric Residence Permit (BRP)** is a physical card historically issued to non-EEA nationals granted UK stay >6 months. It contained your name, date of birth, biometric info, and the photograph taken at your VAC appointment.

### BRP photo

- The photo on the BRP is the one taken **at the VAC** — not the digital photo you uploaded.
- This photo was used for identity verification throughout your stay (bank accounts, renting, proving right to study).
- Students were historically required to collect their BRP within **10 days of arrival** in the UK.

### BRP phase-out — moving to eVisa (2025)

As of 2025, the UK Home Office is phasing out physical BRP cards in favour of a **digital eVisa**:

| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| BRP issuance stopped | Early 2025 |
| Full digital status | All residents expected to hold eVisa only |
| Transition period | Ongoing through 2025–2026 |

#### What this means for new applicants

- If you apply for a Student visa **after 31 October 2024**, you generally will **not** receive a BRP card.
- Instead, you must create a **UKVI account** and access your immigration status digitally via an **eVisa**.
- The photograph taken at your biometric appointment is linked to your digital UKVI account.
- You will still attend a biometric appointment (photo + fingerprints).
- You may still receive a visa vignette (sticker) in your passport for initial entry.
- Keep your UKVI account login credentials safe — they are needed to prove your right to study, work, and rent.

## 5. University Registration & Student ID Photos

After arriving in the UK, every student must complete **university enrolment/registration** — a separate process from the visa application that typically requires a new photograph for your **student ID card**.

### When to upload

- Most UK universities allow online photo upload during pre-registration (before arrival).
- Some take the photo **on-site** during enrolment week.
- A few still accept physical passport-style photos at the registration desk.

### Common requirements (across UK universities)

| Requirement | Typical Specification |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Style | Passport-style: head and shoulders, facing forward |
| Background | Plain light-coloured (varies: white, cream, light grey) |
| Expression | Neutral, mouth closed |
| Eyes | Clearly visible, no hair over eyes |
| Glasses | Check for glare; varies by institution |
| Head covering | Usually allowed for religious/medical reasons if face visible |
| Age of photo | Within last **3–6 months**, matches current appearance |
| Dimensions | Varies (commonly 200×200 px, 300×300 px, or 400×500 px) |
| File format | JPEG or PNG (some accept GIF) |
| File size | Usually under 2 MB |
| Colour | Colour required |

### Where the photo appears

- **Student ID card** (library access, building entry, printing, student discounts)
- **Class registers** and **exam seating** (identity verification by invigilators)
- **Library system** and **virtual learning environment**
- **Campus security** (some buildings use the photo for entry verification)

### Examples from UK universities

| University | Photo Guidance (publicly available) |
|------------|-------------------------------------|
| **Edinburgh** | Passport-style, digital upload via MyEd, good likeness required |
| **Glasgow** | Upload during Academic Registration via MyCampus Student Centre > Personal Information > Photographs |
| **Loughborough** | True likeness, ≤6 months old, plain background, allows natural smile (no teeth) |
| **Birmingham** | High-quality passport-style, head & shoulders, pale background, online upload |
| **Bristol** | Digital upload via Card Services portal; requires surname, UCAS/student number, DOB |

### Tips

- Do not reuse the visa photo (different background requirements).
- Avoid heavy makeup or filters — the photo is used for exam ID.
- Check your university's specific pixel dimensions before cropping.
- Upload high resolution for clear card print.

## 6. CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies)

### What is a CAS?

A **Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS)** is **not** a physical document — it is a unique 14-character alphanumeric reference number generated by your university on the Home Office's Sponsor Management System. The accompanying CAS statement contains details about you, your course, fees, and academic assessment. You need a CAS to apply for a Student Route visa.

### Photo requirements for CAS

- UK universities **may** request a passport-style photograph when issuing the CAS.
- This photo is **digitally attached** to the CAS record in the Sponsor Management System.
- Used by UKVI to verify your identity during visa processing.
- Should **match** the photo used in your visa application.
- Not all universities request a photo — it depends on the institution's internal process.
- If requested, follow UK passport-style standards: 45 mm × 35 mm, light grey/cream background, neutral expression.

## 7. ATAS (Academic Technology Approval Scheme)

### What is ATAS?

The **Academic Technology Approval Scheme** is a UK government clearance scheme administered by the **Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO)** . It applies to students and researchers from certain countries (including Singapore) intending to study or research in sensitive subject areas at postgraduate level (MSc or PhD).

### Subjects requiring ATAS

- Materials science
- Physics (including nuclear physics)
- Engineering (mechanical, aerospace, chemical, electrical, civil — at postgraduate level)
- Computer science (some specialised areas)
- Chemistry and mathematics (some research areas)

**Check your course:** https://www.academic-technology-approval.service.gov.uk/
Your university will provide the CAH3 code for your course.

### ATAS photo requirements

- The application is completed **online** via the UK government portal.
- **Requires** a passport-style photograph upload meeting UK passport standards (same as visa):
  - 45 mm × 35 mm, light grey/cream background, neutral expression, mouth closed.
  - No glasses, no head covering (except religious/medical).
  - Taken within the last 6 months.
- Digital file: JPEG or PNG.

### ATAS process

1. Apply online after receiving an unconditional offer for a relevant course.
2. Upload personal details, passport info, course details, and photo.
3. Processing typically 3–4 weeks (longer in peak periods).
4. ATAS certificate required **before** you can receive your CAS and apply for your visa.
5. Certificate valid for the duration of your course (typically up to 3 years for PhD, 1 year for MSc).

## 8. UK Passport-Style Photo Specifications (Summary Table)

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Size** | **45 mm high × 35 mm wide** (portrait) |
| **Background (visa)** | Plain light grey or cream (**not white**) |
| **Background (passport)** | Plain white or off-white |
| **Expression** | Neutral, mouth closed, no smile |
| **Eyes** | Clearly visible, open, no hair covering — facing directly forward |
| **Glasses** | **Not allowed** (since 2018 rule change) |
| **Head covering** | Only for religious/medical reasons — full face visible, crown to chin |
| **Resolution** | Minimum 600 dpi recommended |
| **Recency** | Within the last **6 months** |
| **Digital format** | JPEG or PNG |
| **File size** | Typically ≤ 1 MB |
| **Colour** | Colour only |
| **Lighting** | Balanced, no shadows on face or background |
| **Head position** | Centred, facing straight ahead, not tilted |
| **Editing** | No filters, no red-eye correction, no airbrushing |

### UK visa vs. Singapore passport photo dimensions

| Feature | UK Visa/Passport | Singapore Passport/IC |
|---------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Dimensions** | 45 mm × 35 mm | 35 mm × 45 mm |
| **Orientation** | Portrait (taller) | Landscape (wider) |
| **Background** | Light grey/cream (visa), white (passport) | White |
| **Glasses** | Not allowed (visa) | Allowed if eyes visible, no glare |
| **Expression** | Neutral, mouth closed | Neutral, mouth closed |

**Important:** Many automated photo booths in Singapore default to 35 mm × 45 mm. For UK applications, the photo must be cropped to 45 mm × 35 mm.

## 10. Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Submitting an old photo** — UKVI requires a new photo for each application (within 6 months). Cannot reuse a photo from a previous visa, even if it was taken recently.
2. **Smiling or showing teeth** — Not allowed. Expression must be completely neutral.
3. **Wrong background colour** — White = UK passport, not UK visa. Visa needs light grey or cream.
4. **Wearing glasses** — Not permitted in UK visa photos since the 2018 rule change.
5. **Heavy makeup, editing, or filters** — Must be a true likeness. No beauty filters, skin smoothing, airbrushing, or Instagram filters.
6. **Shadows on face or background** — Ensure even lighting with no shadows from nose, eyebrows, or head coverings.
7. **Hair covering eyebrows or eyes** — Entire face must be visible from crown of head to chin.
8. **Incorrect dimensions** — UK visa uses 45 mm × 35 mm (portrait). Many applicants submit Singapore standard 35 mm × 45 mm (landscape) by mistake — this causes automatic rejection.
9. **Same photo for visa and student ID** — May be possible but note background colour differences (visa needs grey/cream; many IDs accept white).
10. **Not checking university-specific rules** — Each university sets its own ID photo pixel dimensions and file size limits.

## 11. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Do I need a photo for my UCAS application?**
**A:** No. UCAS does not require or accept photographs. If a university separately requests one, it is handled outside the UCAS system.

**Q2: What size photo does the UK student visa require?**
**A:** 45 mm high × 35 mm wide (portrait orientation).

**Q3: Can I wear glasses in my UK visa photo?**
**A:** No. Glasses are not permitted since the 2018 rule change.

**Q4: What colour background do I need for the visa photo?**
**A:** Plain light grey or cream — not white.

**Q5: How recent does my visa photo need to be?**
**A:** Within the last 6 months, reflecting your current appearance.

**Q6: Do I get a physical BRP card now?**
**A:** As of 2025, physical BRP cards are being phased out. New applicants receive an eVisa (digital status) instead.

**Q7: Where do I go for my biometric appointment in Singapore?**
**A:** VFS Global at 135 Cecil Street, #08-01, Philippine Airlines Building, Singapore 069536.

**Q8: Can I use a smartphone photo for my UK visa application?**
**A:** Yes, as long as it meets all UKVI specifications. Use a UK visa preset app for correct cropping and background.

**Q9: Is ATAS the same as a visa?**
**A:** No. ATAS is a separate clearance certificate for certain sensitive subjects. You need it before receiving your CAS and applying for your visa.

**Q10: What is the difference between a CAS and a visa?**
**A:** CAS is a reference number issued by your university confirming sponsorship. It is required to apply for the visa itself.

**Q11: How much does a UK-compliant visa photo cost in Singapore?**
**A:** ~SGD 6–15 (basic services) to SGD 15–25 (professional studios with digital copy).

**Q12: Can I smile in my student ID photo?**
**A:** Most UK universities ask for neutral expression or natural smile without teeth. Check your institution's guidance.
