## Admission Requirements, GPA Cut-offs, Application Procedures & Degree Pathways

> **Updated:** July 2026 | **Target:** Republic Polytechnic (RP) students & graduates

## 1. Republic Polytechnic's Unique Pedagogy

### Problem-Based Learning (PBL) System

RP is Singapore's only polytechnic using PBL as its sole pedagogy. Instead of lectures, students solve real-world problems in small groups through a weekly cycle: **Day 1 — Problem trigger → Day 2 — Self-directed research & peer learning → Day 3 — Application & presentation**. This produces graduates with strong self-directed learning, teamwork, and problem-solving skills — qualities HK universities value.

### RP's GPA System (4.0 Scale)

| Grade | Point | Grade | Point |
|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| A+/A  | 4.0   | C+    | 2.5   |
| B+    | 3.5   | C     | 2.0   |
| B     | 3.0   | D+/D  | 1.5-1.0 |

RP awards: **Distinction** (cGPA ≥ 3.5), **Merit** (≥ 3.0), **Pass** (≥ 1.0).

### How HK Universities View PBL

HK universities are **familiar with Singapore's system** and accept RP diplomas alongside NP/SP/TP/NYP. PBL is well-regarded — admissions officers see it as producing strong communicators and researchers. RP's GPA maps directly to HK's 4.0 scale: 3.8-4.0 = highly competitive, 3.5-3.7 = competitive, 3.0-3.4 = meets minimum for many programmes, < 2.8 = limited options.

## 4. Admission Systems: JUPAS vs Non-JUPAS

**RP graduates apply via Non-JUPAS** — NOT JUPAS.

| | JUPAS | Non-JUPAS |
|---|-------|-----------|
| Who | HK local students (HKDSE) | International & diploma holders |
| RP grads | ❌ Not eligible | ✅ Eligible |
| Method | Centralised system | Direct to each university separately |
| Deadlines | Fixed schedule | Varies by university (Oct-Feb) |

### How Non-JUPAS Works

1. Apply directly to **each university separately** (no centralised system)
2. Each university has its own online portal
3. You can apply to **multiple universities simultaneously**
4. Review is **holistic**: GPA, personal statement, recommendations, portfolio, interview

### Early Round vs Main Round

| Round | Typical Deadline | Decisions | Strategy |
|-------|----------------|-----------|----------|
| **Early** | Nov-Dec (Y3 Sem 1) | Dec-Feb | Apply if GPA ≥ 3.3-3.5 |
| **Main** | Jan-Feb (Y3 Sem 2) | Mar-Jun | Most applicants apply here |
| **Late** | Mar-Jun | Jun-Aug | Remaining places |

Applications rejected early are **automatically reconsidered** in the main round at most universities.

### Required Documents

1. **Official RP transcripts** (in English)
2. **Diploma certificate** or expected graduation letter
3. **IELTS/TOEFL scores**
4. **Personal statement** (500-1,000 words)
5. **Recommendation letters** (1-2 from RP lecturers)
6. **Passport/IC copy**
7. **Portfolio** (Design, Architecture, Creative Media programmes)
8. **Application fee** (HKD 400-500 per university)
9. **Internship certificates, awards, co-curricular records**

### How PBL Curriculum is Assessed

HK universities evaluate RP transcripts via:
1. **Course-by-course mapping** — comparing module descriptions
2. **Content relevance** — close matches get credit
3. **Grade standardisation** — B (3.0) or above typically needed
4. **Programme-specific** — Engineering/Business have most overlap

**💡 Maximise credit:** Apply PolyU Senior Year Entry; provide detailed RP module synopses; highlight relevant internships; contact the credit transfer office early.

## 8. Application Timeline

### Year 2, Sem 2 (Jan-Apr)
- **Jan-Feb:** Research HK universities & programmes. Shortlist 3-5 targets
- **Mar-Apr:** Identify module alignment. Collect RP syllabi

### Year 3, Sem 1 (May-Oct)
- **May-Jun:** Register for IELTS/TOEFL. Begin prep
- **Jun-Jul:** Take IELTS/TOEFL (aim for 6.5+/90+)
- **Jul-Aug:** Draft personal statement. Request recommendation letters
- **Aug-Sep:** Request official RP transcripts (allow 2-4 weeks). Prepare portfolio (Design/Architecture/Media)
- **Sep-Oct:** Research scholarships. Finalise shortlist

### Year 3, Sem 2 (Nov-Feb) — Application Period
- **Early Nov:** CUHK Early Round deadline
- **Mid-Nov:** HKUST, PolyU, CityU Early Round deadlines
- **Late Nov:** HKU Early Round deadline (last Wed of Nov, noon HKT)
- **Dec:** Early round interviews begin
- **Mid-Jan:** CUHK, HKUST, CityU Main Round deadlines
- **Early Feb:** PolyU Main Round (Senior Year) deadline
- **Late Feb:** Last chance for rolling admissions

### Post-Graduation (Mar-Aug)
- **Mar-Apr:** Receive & compare offers
- **Apr-May:** Accept offer. Apply for **Hong Kong Student Visa**
- **May-Jun:** Apply for housing. Book flights. Arrange finances
- **Jun-Jul:** Obtain student visa (4-6 weeks processing)
- **Jul-Aug:** Attend orientation. Arrive in HK
- **Late Aug/Sep:** Semester begins

### Student Visa Process
1. University guides you after offer acceptance
2. Submit: passport copy, acceptance letter, financial proof, form ID995A
3. Processing: **4-6 weeks** | Fee: HKD 230-550
4. Receive Visa/Entry Permit Label
5. Register for HKID within 30 days of arrival

## 9. Hong Kong vs Singapore Universities

| Factor | Hong Kong 🇭🇰 | Singapore 🇸🇬 |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Entry difficulty** | Lower for RP grads (GPA 3.0-3.5 viable) | Much harder (NUS/NTU need 3.8+) |
| **Admission probability** | Higher at PolyU/CityU/HKBU/LU | Low for NUS/NTU/SMU |
| **Tuition/yr** | SGD 24,000-38,000 | SGD 8,200-11,200 (subsidised) |
| **Total yearly cost** | SGD 44,000-75,000 | SGD 20,000-33,000 |
| **Duration** | 2-4 years (2 yrs via Senior Year at PolyU) | 2-3 years (SIT/SUSS) |
| **Post-grad work** | IANG visa: 12 months to seek employment | Automatic for PR/citizens; EP for foreigners |
| **PR path** | 7 years continuous → HK Permanent Resident | Varies |
| **Language** | English instruction; daily Cantonese | Comfortable multilingual |
| **Global recognition** | 4 unis in QS top 100 | NUS/NTU top tier; SIT/SUSS less known |

### Pros of HK for RP Graduates
- ✅ **More accessible** for mid-range GPAs (3.0-3.5)
- ✅ World-class universities with international recognition
- ✅ IANG visa = flexible post-graduation employment
- ✅ Gateway to China careers
- ✅ Senior Year entry at PolyU/CityU = 2-year degree completion
- ✅ Many scholarships for non-local students

### Cons of HK
- ❌ Higher tuition than Singapore subsidised fees
- ❌ Cantonese dominant in daily life (English works but limited)
- ❌ 3.5-4 hour flight from Singapore
- ❌ Less familiar with RP's PBL system (needs explanation)
- ❌ Different political/social environment

### Pros of Staying in SG
- ✅ Familiar environment, proximity to family
- ✅ Lower tuition (subsidised)
- ✅ Strong local employment
- ✅ No language barrier

### Cons of Staying in SG
- ❌ Very competitive entry into autonomous universities
- ❌ Limited pathways (SIT/SUSS or private unis)
- ❌ SIT/SUSS degrees less internationally recognised than HK top universities

## 10. Application Tips & Success Profile

### GPA Targets by University Tier

| Tier | Target GPA | Universities |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Top | ≥ 3.7 | HKU, HKUST (Business/Computing) |
| Mid | ≥ 3.3 | CUHK, HKUST (Eng/Science), PolyU (competitive) |
| Entry | ≥ 2.8-3.0 | PolyU, CityU, HKBU, LU, EdUHK |

### Application Strengthening Tips

1. **Personal Statement** — Explain WHY Hong Kong specifically. Connect PBL training to your chosen field. Show programme-specific knowledge.

2. **Recommendations** — Choose RP lecturers who know your PBL work. Give 1 month notice. Ask them to highlight your problem-solving, teamwork, and research skills.

3. **Portfolio (Design/Architecture/Media)** — RP's PBL projects are excellent portfolio material. Include 10-15 strong projects. Show process AND final outcome. Highlight both group (teamwork) and individual components.

4. **Internships** — RP's compulsory 3-month internship is valuable evidence of practical experience. Use it in your personal statement.

5. **IELTS/TOEFL** — Aim for 6.5-7.0 (IELTS) or 90-100 (TOEFL). Higher scores strengthen competitive programme applications.

6. **Extracurriculars** — Leadership roles, community service, competitions, RP clubs/CCA show well-roundedness.

7. **Apply Early** — Early Round applications show strong interest and get priority consideration.

8. **Interview Prep** — Many programmes interview (especially Business, Medicine, Design). Prepare to discuss: PBL methodology, why HK, career goals, your portfolio.

### Profile of a Successful RP → HK Applicant
- **GPA ≥ 3.5** (3.7+ for HKU/HKUST)
- **IELTS 7.0+** or TOEFL 100+
- **Strong personal statement** connecting PBL to HK's industry
- **Relevant internship** in their field
- **Competition participation** (case competitions, hackathons)
- **Leadership role** in RP club/organisation
- **Clear career goals** aligned with HK economy (finance, tech, logistics, design)
